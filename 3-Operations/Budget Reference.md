# Budget Reference

Full budget reference table from 37 Carl-Claire calls. Use this before commissioning any creative work or vendor. If a quote falls substantially outside these ranges, demand a detailed justification — or walk away.

> "If somebody quotes me 500, another one 2000, I need to understand the technical differences to make a decision." (Call 19)

## Budget Philosophy

**Rule 1: Never scale before proof.**

> "You have to test people bit by bit, challenge by challenge. And yeah, if somebody can handle a hundred thousand dollar budget a year, then he should have it. But first he will have the way to prove you." (Call 28)

**Rule 2: Reward creative initiative.**

> "If you have five, six of those every two months, because where you come up with cool shit... you could charge $300 of creative and design fee or concept fee on top of what you're doing." (Call 11)

**Rule 3: The last 20% is everything.**

"The difference between 80% and 100% is massive for a luxury brand." For Prestige-tier products, never compromise on photography, design, or materials quality.

**Rule 4: Wearability = marketing ROI.**

> "The difference between you burning money on something stupid and you actually generating free marketing value is whether people are going to wear it or not." (Call 1)

Merchandise that doesn't get worn is burning money. The standard: Would Carl wear this at his house in Monaco?

**Rule 5: No hidden extras.**

All-inclusive pricing only. Administrative fees, communication fees, and small add-ons on top of an agreed creative fee are unacceptable. (Call 37)

## Red Flags in Vendor Quotes

Watch for these behaviors:

- Quote arrives with no technical breakdown
- Price is significantly above or below market range without explanation
- Hidden extras appear after agreement (communication fees, admin fees)
- Vendor disappears when asked to justify their quote
- Price inflated by 100% from previous agreed rate

> "She inflated the prices of everything by 100%... all inclusive." (Call 37)
