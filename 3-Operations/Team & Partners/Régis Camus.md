# Régis Camus

## Who He Is

Régis Camus is the most decorated sparkling winemaker in history and the creative soul behind every HEAVENSAKE expression. He is the first champagne master to apply the art of French assemblage to Japanese sake.

- **Title:** Cellar Master & Co-Founder, HEAVENSAKE
- **Previous:** Cellar Master, Piper-Heidsieck & Charles Heidsieck (1994–2018); Cellar Master, Rare Champagne (2018–2022)
- **Awards:** 8-time IWC "Sparkling Winemaker of the Year" (2004, 2006, 2007, 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011, 2013) — a record that has never been matched
- **Education:** Oenology, University of Reims
- **Career start:** Mid-1970s, Champagne co-operative; later president of the Regional Union of Winemakers in Champagne

---

## Career at Piper-Heidsieck & Charles Heidsieck

Régis joined Piper-Heidsieck in 1994, working alongside the legendary Daniel Thibault. After Thibault's untimely death in 2002, Régis became cellar master of both Piper-Heidsieck and Charles Heidsieck — managing two distinct house styles from the same source materials.

His innovations at Piper:
- **Sublime** (2001) — a demi-sec with extended aging, unusual for the period
- **Sauvage** — a deeply-hued rosé with substantial Pinot Noir vinified as still red wine, ahead of the climate-change trend
- **Cuvée Florens Louis** — created exclusively for duty free and travel retail
- **Rare Vintage 1998** — won the Daniel Thibault Trophy for Champion Sparkling Wine at IWC 2012 and 2013

His philosophy at Heidsieck: *"It's a privilege to make both — because they must stay separate in style, but both come from the same source materials."* ([Wine Spectator](https://www.winespectator.com/articles/rare-champagne-s-regis-camus-bids-adieu-his-legacy-secure))

In 2015, Cyril Brun took over Charles Heidsieck. In 2018, Émilien Boutillat took over Piper-Heidsieck. Régis focused exclusively on Rare Champagne, transforming it into a maison in its own right.

*"Since 2018, I have worked with Maud Rabin to turn Rare Champagne into a maison in its own right. Appreciated, respected and sought-after, Rare Champagne has become the jewel I was hoping for."* — Régis Camus

He officially retired from Rare Champagne in March 2022.

## His Role at HEAVENSAKE

Régis is not a consultant or a name on the bottle. He is the creative author of every HEAVENSAKE expression. His role is Assemblage Master — he personally travels to each brewery, tastes dozens of sake batches, and composes the final blend.

### The Assemblage Process

*"Creating sake through assemblage is like composing a symphony — combining single elements to reveal something greater than the sum of its parts. I work closely with the Toji masters of the breweries to craft blends that express elevation, elegance, and a certain joie de vivre."*

*"The process is a true dialogue between the brew masters and myself. A dance between respect, passion, and creation leading to a moment of bliss where we know we found the perfect assemblage that embodies the spirit of the brewery and the soul of HEAVENSAKE."*

His process:
1. Visits each brewery partner in Japan
2. Works directly with the Toji (master brewer) — this is a dialogue, not a directive
3. Tastes multiple sake batches — different tanks, different rice polishing ratios, different fermentation profiles
4. Composes the final assemblage — blending batches to create a "third color" (see [[Brand Story — The Assemblage]])
5. Fine-tunes layer by layer — nose, taste, mouthfeel, length — like composing music

The key distinction: **Assemblage is not blending.** Blending is industrial — mixing to standardize. Assemblage is artistic — combining to elevate. See [[Never Say List]].

## Why Régis Matters for the Brand

1. **Credibility bridge:** He connects HEAVENSAKE to the world of Champagne and fine wine — a world the target audience already understands and respects
2. **The "why" of assemblage:** Without Régis, assemblage is just a marketing word. With him, it's a 50-year career applied to a new medium
3. **Wine glass ritual:** Régis is the reason HEAVENSAKE is served in a wine glass — he designed the sake to breathe and express itself the way great Champagne does
4. **Proof over promise:** 8 IWC titles is not an opinion. It's the most objective measure of winemaking excellence in the world, applied to sake

### In Communications

- Always name him specifically: **"Régis Camus"** — never "our cellar master" or "a French winemaker"
- Lead with the IWC record when speaking to wine audiences
- Lead with the Toji dialogue when speaking to sake audiences
- His Champagne pedigree is particularly powerful for the **Champagne Switcher** persona (see [[Buyer Personas]])
