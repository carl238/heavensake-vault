## SLIDE 2: JAPANESE EXCELLENCE. FRENCH TASTE.

HEAVENSAKE is a composition by acclaimed French cellar master, Régis Camus, in collaboration with Japan's most respected sake breweries.

Admired for his assemblage virtuosity, Camus has been named IWC "Winemaker of the Year" eight times.

His respectful ambition is to create unique sake showcasing the art of assemblage while honoring the magnificent heritage of Japanese sake-making.

---

## SLIDE 3: BRINGING ASSEMBLAGE TO SAKE

Régis Camus likens assemblage to musical creation, where single elements are harmoniously layered to create enchanting compositions.

Through years of experience, Régis understands how different aromas and flavors interact with each other to bring new taste profiles to life.

When HEAVENSAKE first brought assemblage to sake, some people mistook it for the more common act of "blending," which was never intended to elevate the sake experience.

**As such HEAVENSAKE was the first to bring assemblage to sake.**

## SLIDE 5–6: OUR DISTINGUISHED BREWERY PARTNERS

### NOGUCHI
The Noguchi Naohiko Sake Institute is a tribute to a legend: Naohiko Noguchi, "The God of Sake Brewing."

Noguchi revived the Yamahai method and spent over 70 years crafting sake with one goal: making people happy.

### NIIZAWA
Iwao Niizawa, brewery's fifth-generation president, passed on the torch as a brewmaster to Japan's youngest female brewmaster Nanami Watanabe.

Niizawa produces "Absolute 0" — the world's most polished sake.

### SHICHIKEN
Founded in 1750, it blends tradition with innovation, most notably through its refined sparkling sakes, including a collaboration with Chef Alain Ducasse. With a legacy touched by Emperor Meiji and a commitment to sustainability, Shichiken embodies the pinnacle of Japanese craftsmanship and modern luxury.

### URAKASUMI
Founded in 1724, Urakasumi provides the sacred sake for the Shiogama Shrine. The 13th generation of the Saura family manages this brewery. Saura-san is also the founder of the Sake Samurai Association promoting sake all around the world.

### DEWAZAKURA
Established in 1892, Yamagata Prefecture's Dewazakura sake brewery kick-started a national trend for Ginjo-style sake with the 1980 release of their signature Oka Ginjo. The success of this brew was such that, for many, Ginjo remains synonymous with Dewazakura.

### KONISHI
Located in the Hyogo Prefecture, Konishi is the oldest single-family brewery dating back to 1550. Its President, Shintarō Konishi, is the 15th generation managing the business.
