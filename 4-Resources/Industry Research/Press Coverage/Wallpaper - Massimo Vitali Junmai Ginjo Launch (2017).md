# Massimo Vitali Reaches New Heights at HeavenSake

**Publication:** Wallpaper*  
**Date:** March 2017 (archived/updated July 31, 2022)  
**Author:** Rosa Bertoli  
**URL:** https://www.wallpaper.com/lifestyle/massimo-vitali-captures-crowd-heavensake-jumai-ginjo-party

---

## Article Summary

Wallpaper*'s second major piece on HEAVENSAKE, covering the brand's Junmai Ginjo launch event at the American Cathedral in Paris during Paris Fashion Week 2017. The piece is notable for its emphasis on HEAVENSAKE's cultural events strategy — the "A Better High" series — and the collaboration with Urakasumi for the new expression. Renowned crowd photographer Massimo Vitali was literally suspended in the air above 1,000 guests to capture the scene.

---

## Key Brand Description by Wallpaper*

> *"Launched last year, Franco-Japanese sake label HeavenSake positions itself as an alternative to champagne."*

> *"The founders are adamant their brand is not just about serving and drinking sake."*

---

## Key Details

- **New product launched:** HeavenSake Junmai Ginjo — Régis Camus × Urakasumi (£45 for 720ml)
- **Event:** "A Better High" series, No. 2 — held at the American Cathedral in Paris
- **Event produced by:** Etienne Russo and Villa Eugénie
- **Highlights:** 
  - Over 1,000 party guests
  - At 11:45pm, all attendees donned black pollution masks as a climate change awareness performance
  - Large-scale faux bonsai tree suspended over the altar/DJ booth
  - Massimo Vitali elevated 5 metres above crowd to photograph
  - DJ sets by Black Coffee and Agoria; Japanese drummers Yamato performed live

---

## Brand Events Philosophy (from article)

Each launch is accompanied by a carefully staged "cultural event" under the "A Better High" series title. The brand eschews traditional tasting rituals while celebrating:
- Multidisciplinary craft
- Showmanship
- Artistic performances
- Gastronomy
- Contemporary music and fashion

---

## Brand Perception Analysis

Wallpaper*'s second HEAVENSAKE feature reinforces the brand's identity as a **cultural movement**, not just a drinks label. The article's emphasis on the event (rather than tasting notes) reflects how both the publication and the brand see HEAVENSAKE: as a lifestyle and cultural phenomenon rooted in the intersection of Japanese craft and French creative energy.

The choice to host at the American Cathedral in Paris — a storied neoclassical building — and to use Massimo Vitali (famous for monumental crowd photography) signals aspirations toward cultural iconography. Wallpaper* treats this seriously, positioning HEAVENSAKE in the same cultural conversation as art galleries and fashion houses.
