## Key Facts from Article

- **Breweries:** Dassai, Urakasumi, Konishi, Hakushika (at time of publication)
- **All expressions:** Junmai class (pure rice, no distilled alcohol or added sugar)
- **All-natural:** Sulfite-free, gluten-free, additive-free, 3× less acidic than wine
- **Serve:** Chilled, in wine glass
- **Celebrity fans:** Neil Patrick Harris, Dita Von Teese, A$AP Rocky
- **Restaurant placement:** Acclaimed restaurants and hotels across the US
- **Retail:** Whole Foods, BevMo!, Trader Joe's

## Brand Perception Analysis

7x7's framing is the most accessible and commercial of all HEAVENSAKE coverage found: the "Champagne of sake" headline is a direct, clear value proposition that any reader can understand in seconds. The celebrity name-drops (Dita Von Teese, A$AP Rocky, Neil Patrick Harris) signal cultural cross-pollination — this is a brand that attracts figures from multiple creative worlds.

The retail distribution note (Whole Foods, BevMo!, Trader Joe's) alongside Michelin restaurant placement reveals HEAVENSAKE's unusual market positioning: accessible enough for mainstream retail, prestigious enough for the world's best restaurants. Very few brands operate credibly at both extremes simultaneously.

For San Francisco's luxury lifestyle readership — who know the local sake scene well, given the city's proximity to Japanese culture — the two consecutive SF IWC wins carry particular local weight.
