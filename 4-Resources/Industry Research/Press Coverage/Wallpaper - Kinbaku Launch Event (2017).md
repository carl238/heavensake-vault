# In Suspense: HeavenSake Marks Its Debut with a Powerful Kinbaku Performance

**Publication:** Wallpaper*  
**Date:** March 2017 (archived online September 11, 2022)  
**Author:** Harriet Lloyd Smith  
**URL:** https://www.wallpaper.com/gallery/art/heavensake-launches-in-paris-with-kinbaku-japanese-bondage-performance

---

## Article Summary

Wallpaper*'s gallery piece covering HEAVENSAKE's inaugural "Better High" event series — specifically the first event which featured a performance of kinbaku (Japanese rope bondage/art form) by Japanese master Kinoko Hajime, as well as the second event featuring Massimo Vitali suspended above the crowd to photograph it. The article captures the brand's deliberately provocative and boundary-pushing event aesthetic.

---

## Key Brand Description by Wallpaper*

> *"Franco-Japanese luxury drinks brand HeavenSake recently launched its second sake at a 'cultural gathering' in Paris, culminating in renowned photographer Massimo Vitali being suspended high over the heaving crowd to capture the scene."*

---

## Key Event Details

**First "Better High" Event:**
- Performance: kinbaku (literally "the beauty of tight binding") by Japanese master Kinoko Hajime
- Audience reaction: "At times disturbing, the session left the predominantly European and American audience at the event speechless"
- Wallpaper*'s observation: "The practice and its more spectacular iterations create a bridge between past and present, merging craft with exhibitionism and sexual performance in a way that is often difficult to understand and dissect for the untrained viewer"

**Second Event:**
- Renowned photographer Massimo Vitali suspended high above crowd
- Second sake launch (Junmai Ginjo) celebrated simultaneously

---

## Brand Perception Analysis

Wallpaper* frames HEAVENSAKE's event strategy as genuinely confrontational cultural programming — not safe corporate entertainment. The kinbaku performance is treated seriously as an art form, and the audience's speechlessness is presented as a desired outcome.

This coverage positions HEAVENSAKE as a brand willing to deploy **authentic Japanese cultural forms** — including ones that are challenging or unfamiliar to Western audiences — as part of its artistic identity. Wallpaper* respects and amplifies this, treating it as the mark of a brand with genuine cultural ambitions rather than superficial lifestyle branding.

The recurring Wallpaper* coverage of HEAVENSAKE events (three separate pieces) demonstrates that the publication sees the brand as a reliable source of culturally interesting content — an unusual level of editorial trust for a drinks label.
