## Article Summary

A Q&A interview with Nes Rueda, HEAVENSAKE's Managing Director for the US market, published in The Hype Magazine (a culture, music, and lifestyle publication). The interview covers brand origin story, what makes HEAVENSAKE different from other sakes, bottle design philosophy, food pairing recommendations, and 2020 plans. It contains some of the most detailed direct quotes from brand leadership about the product's design, mission, and market positioning.

---

## Key Brand Description

> *"When renowned champagne winemaker Régis Camus set out to collaborate with three revered sake breweries to create HEAVENSAKE, he was already breaking centuries-old traditions."*

> *"By bringing a champagne-maker's sensibility to the process, he created a selection of dry-style sakes that prove how 'Japan's drink' has much more pairing acumen than just the obvious."*

> *"HEAVENSAKE's three expressions on the market — Junmai 12, Junmai Ginjo and Junmai Daiginjo — all offer fresh, fruity, floral flavor profiles that shine against umami and amino acid-rich foods and can even hold up to spicy flavors."*

---

## Key Quotes: Nes Rueda, Managing Director

**On concept:**
> *"Our founder was looking for better drinking alternatives. While wine is timeless, the growing health consciousness around the world prompted our exploration into sake. The simplicity and purity of the ingredients in sake allows consumers to indulge without many of the negative consequences of most alcohols. HEAVENSAKE is sulfite and gluten free. It has acidity lower than white wine and no added sugars. It's so pure that almost any allergy can still be satisfied. All of this combined with its refreshing and versatile taste led HEAVENSAKE. Hence our tagline #ABetterHigh."*

**On first-time consumer experience:**
> *"Most people have only had 'hot sake,' nigoris — 'cloudy sake' — or sake bombs. So when people experience HEAVENSAKE in a stemmed wine glass, they are blown away by the flavor profile, refinement and versatility of it."*

**On differentiation:**
> *"HEAVENSAKE is special in many ways. We wanted to make a sake that was world-class without compromising quality or tradition, only collaborating with the best breweries in Japan. By the way, everything is 100% made in Japan — bottle, cap, cap wrapper, liquid — then shipped in refrigerated casks, so you literally drink what leaves the brewery. The other major difference is Regis Camus, 8-time winner of the IWC Winemaker of the Year award, he works in tandem with the Toji's (brewmaster) and uses champagne technique to blend our sakes. This gives HEAVENSAKE an unprecedented roundness, texture and refinement."*

**On bottle design:**
> *"This is also a Franco-Japanese collaboration. We used an agency in Paris who works on iconic Champagne brands (Dom P, Ruinart) for the bottle design. The rakkan (logo) and font were designed by a well-known Art Director, Yotsugi Yasunori. The proprietary shape of the HEAVENSAKE bottle was designed to evoke the purity of water, the timelessness of wine and the character of champagne. The seal (rakkan) — the HEAVENSAKE rakkan — is a reference to our Franco-Japanese heritage. The hexagonal shape representing France, commonly referred to as 'L'hexagone' due to the singular shape of the country. The irregular lines, inspired by Japanese calligraphy (kanji), create 3 hexagons for water, rice and koji."*

**On food pairings:**
> *"Everything, it really depends what you are in the mood for. For our Junmai 12, I think of rice dishes, mushrooms, black truffle, lean beefs, anything tempura or fried. Our Junmai Ginjo goes exceptionally well with raw fish, so this is your sake for nigiri and sashimi. It also pairs beautifully with tomato dishes like pizza and pasta! The Junmai Daiginjo goes well with Parmigiano Reggiano, Pata Negra, duck, wagyu beef, lobster, caviar, white truffle, uni — you get the idea."*

**On proudest accomplishment:**
> *"From selling the brand's first bottle to being named 'Best Sake' at the 2019 San Francisco International Wine Competition, to walking into a restaurant like Sushi Nakazawa or CATCH and seeing HEAVENSAKE on people's tables."*

## Brand Perception Analysis

The Hype Magazine reaches an urban, culture-forward audience — music fans, fashion followers, lifestyle enthusiasts who may not be wine experts but are interested in elevated consumption experiences. The interview validates HEAVENSAKE's crossover appeal: this is not a publication that covers specialist sake, it covers cultural cool.

Nes Rueda's quotes in this interview represent some of the clearest articulations of HEAVENSAKE's brand differentiation: the cold-chain shipping (100% integrity from brewery to glass), the rakkan's dual France/Japan symbolism, and the Daiginjo's pairing with caviar, wagyu, and white truffle position HEAVENSAKE squarely in ultra-luxury territory.

The 2019 SF IWC win being cited as a "proudest accomplishment" confirms this was a watershed moment for the brand's third-party credibility.
