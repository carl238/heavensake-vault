# Heavensake's Vision for the World's Most Beautiful Omakase

**Publication:** Surface Magazine  
**Date:** June 10, 2025  
**Author:** David Graver  
**URL:** https://www.surfacemag.com/articles/heavensake-omakase/

---

## Article Summary

Surface Magazine — a leading design, art, and luxury culture publication — features HEAVENSAKE's bespoke omakase event in Monaco, staged by Carl Hirschmann to celebrate the Noguchi Prestige release. The article positions HEAVENSAKE as an experience brand at the intersection of dining, design, and Japanese cultural heritage. The mirrored table suspended above the Mediterranean is treated as an artwork in its own right.

---

## Key Brand Description by Surface

> *"Rarely do all the senses align for one meal — a culinary delight set into a carefully designed scene where texture, scent, and sound have been meticulously orchestrated."*

> *"Heavensake founder and creative director Carl Hirschmann hosts the world's most beautiful omakase to celebrate the Noguchi Prestige release."*

> *"Elegant and elevated, the liquid carries citrus notes that introduce a profile of tropical fruit flavors."*

---

## Key Quotes: Carl Hirschmann

> *"Heavensake has always been about sharing the beauty of Japanese culture with the world."*

> *"I had a very clear idea about the design of the event. I said, 'Look, I want to celebrate omakase culture.' This came about because I understood the theatrical beauty of it. I knew the power of that."*

> *"The mirror in the open air expresses elevation by mirroring the sky, but then you have the mirror expressing purity by reflecting your soul. You see yourself in the depths."*

> *"I took inspiration from a Japanese sculptor and ceramic master named Fukami Sueharu. His cups changed my life. With each cup, he explores the beauty of imperfection. They have two different treatments — one is layered with glaze, the other is more raw. I wanted to share these two different sensory experiences with people."*

> *"We have access to all these great people that want to work with us. We knew we needed to create something unique. And in that process, through my friends, I was lucky enough to be introduced to the Arita Porcelain Lab, and the owner of this company, Satoru Matsumoto, who is the seventh generation head of the Yazaemon Kiln."*

> *"It was always a dream of ours to work with Noguchi Naohiko, the man the Japanese call 'the god of sake brewing.' He opened up his personal archive of vintage sake to us — something he never let anybody access."*

> *"I was able to create an omakase dream. The level of creativity, the level of excellence, the level of attention to detail was all there. There were so many layers to it. One part was aesthetically simple, another was almost philosophical."*

## Event Details

- **Location:** Helicopter pad at Carl Hirschmann's home in Monaco, transformed into an outdoor dining space
- **Table design:** Mirrored table suspended above the Mediterranean
- **Chef:** Locally acclaimed Monaco omakase chef sourced through connections
- **Inspiration:** Fukami Sueharu, Japanese sculptor and ceramic master
- **Bottle collaboration:** Arita Porcelain Lab — Satoru Matsumoto, 7th generation head of Yazaemon Kiln

## Brand Perception Analysis

Surface Magazine's feature cements HEAVENSAKE's positioning as a **design and luxury culture brand**, not merely a drinks company. The article barely discusses the liquid itself — it's primarily about the aesthetic philosophy of the event, the design of the bottle, and Hirschmann's vision of omakase as a total sensory experience.

This is exactly the kind of editorial coverage that distinguishes ultra-premium brands: when a publication of Surface's stature writes about your event as a work of art, you have crossed from "luxury product" to "cultural institution."

Surface frames HEAVENSAKE through design-world language: imperfection, texture, reflection, philosophy. This positions the brand alongside luxury houses like Hermès or Aesop that are as much about aesthetic philosophy as they are about functional products.
