# Why This Award-Winning Champagne Maker Is Turning to Sake

**Publication:** Fortune  
**Date:** October 20, 2019  
**Author:** Shana Clarke  
**URL:** https://fortune.com/longform/heavensake-champagne-regis-camus-sake/

---

## Article Summary

Fortune's longform feature on Régis Camus and the HEAVENSAKE project, published in their longform editorial section. The article covers Camus's career arc — from Piper-Heidsieck to Maison Rare to HEAVENSAKE — and explains the assemblage methodology in depth. It includes rare quotes from a Dassai spokesperson validating the collaboration, and frames HEAVENSAKE as a bridge for Western palates into the sake world.

---

## Key Brand Description by Fortune

> *"The new project, called Heavensake, relies heavily on the concept of blending, considered an art in Champagne, and aims to create a bridge for Western palates into the world of sake."*

> *"Heavensake departs from traditional bottles and uses teardrop-shaped vessels that are, frankly, quite sexy."*

> *"The brand highlights many of sake's inherent qualities—gluten-free, low acid, no added sugar—and repackages the message for today's wellness-focused consumer."*

---

## Key Quotes

### Régis Camus

> *"My first sake tasting was a Junmai sake, which are simply brewed with no added white spirit alcohol in it. Junmai means 'pure' in Japanese. This experience changed the perception I originally had of the harsh, strong sake that are served hot."*

> *"The first step for me in creating Heavensake was to define a house style. To me, this means elegance, delicacy, freshness, and minerality. On the palate, floral and fruity notes with a long, lingering taste."*

> *"Champagne is not one plus one equals two. Union is the force of a great Champagne, blending provides different layers, which felt the best to me—and may be the only way to create the house style I previously described. You need multiple layers to create this effect and sensation on the nose and palate."*

> *"Each brewery gave me a large panel of batch samples; some were made with Yamada Nishiki rice, other with Kura No Hana rice, other with Toyonoshiki rice. The rice polishing ratio was sometimes different, or it was the yeast used during the fermentation."*

> *"The tojis in Japan are the experts at the technicality of making the individual part or batch. I focus on combining the lots and creating balance."*

> *"I wanted to introduce something I have been doing for several decades in Champagne, and quite successfully, to the sake world."*

### Shohei Yamani, Dassai International Strategy Department

> *"We decided to participate in the project with Heavensake because we sympathized with their passion: to promote sake to people who have not yet tried it. Indeed, we believe in the long-term benefit and great potential. [Their project is] broadening the international market for sake, which enables us to expand markets and the potential for the future."*

## Brand Perception Analysis

Fortune's framing of HEAVENSAKE is ambitious and substantive: this is a **career-defining project** by a world-class winemaker, not a side hustle. The longform format signals Fortune sees this as a significant business and cultural story worth deep investigation.

Key perception points:
1. **Credibility anchored in Camus's career** — described as an eight-time "Sparkling Winemaker of the Year" — the founder's credentials are treated as the brand's most compelling asset
2. **Wellness-forward framing** — Fortune notes the brand is smart to repackage sake's inherent health qualities (low acid, no sulfites, gluten-free) for modern consumers
3. **Dassai's own validation** — the Dassai spokesperson quote is remarkable: Japan's most prestigious sake house actively endorses HEAVENSAKE's mission as aligned with their own international ambitions
4. **Bottle design called "sexy"** — Fortune's language here is unusually effusive, suggesting genuine product appreciation beyond marketing copy

The article's placement as a Fortune *longform* (not a standard news brief) confirms HEAVENSAKE was already considered a serious business story in 2019.
