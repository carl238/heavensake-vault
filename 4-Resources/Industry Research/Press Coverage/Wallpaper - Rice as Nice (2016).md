# Rice as Nice: HeavenSake Makes Waves in the Luxury Drinks Market

**Publication:** Wallpaper* (W*206)  
**Date:** May 2016 (originally published in print issue W*206; later archived online)  
**Author:** Rosa Bertoli  
**URL:** https://www.wallpaper.com/lifestyle/parisian-sake-brand-heavensake-set-to-rival-champagne

---

## Article Summary

Wallpaper*'s flagship profile of HEAVENSAKE, originally appearing in their May 2016 print issue — making it one of the **earliest major luxury press features** on the brand. The article introduces HEAVENSAKE to Wallpaper*'s design- and luxury-conscious readership as a serious challenger in the premium drinks market. It covers the brand's founding story, its Franco-Japanese collaboration with Dassai, the role of Régis Camus, and the philosophy behind the sleek bottle design.

---

## Key Brand Description by Wallpaper*

> *"Move over champagne, a new Parisian sake brand may soon be the toast of the luxury market."*

> *"Franco-Japanese collaboration HeavenSake are making waves in the luxury drinks market, by creating a non-acidic, easy-drinking alternative to champagne."*

---

## Key Quotes

### Carl Hirschmann

> *"Drinking acidic wines, champagnes or sodas was problematic for me. I discovered that Junmai Daiginjo sake [a pure type of sake, with no added alcohol] was three times less acidic than wine, so I stuck to sake."*

### Etienne Russo (co-founder, Villa Eugénie)

> *"Sake is water-based, and its alcohol level is close to wine or champagne. We wanted the bottle to reflect these elements: the purity of water, the agelessness of wine and the character of champagne."*

> *"One imagines heaven as pure, light, beautiful, elegant and inaccessible. What more does a luxury brand want?"*

## Product Details from Article

**HeavenSake Junmai Daiginjo** (launch expression, brewed by Dassai, Yamaguchi prefecture)  
- Fragrance: lily, iris, lilac, hyacinth, jasmine, herbal notes  
- Taste: suave and fruity  
- Bottle: sleek, black — designed by Hirschmann, Eymere and Russo to evoke purity of water, agelessness of wine, character of champagne

## Brand Origin Details

- **Founders:** Carl Hirschmann (businessman, sake connoisseur), Benjamin Eymere (publisher), Etienne Russo (founder, Villa Eugénie fashion show production)
- **Winemaker/Blender:** Régis Camus — winemaker of Piper-Heidsieck since 1994
- **First brewery partner:** Dassai (Yamaguchi prefecture)
- **Brand positioning:** Alternative to champagne for art and fashion events

## Brand Perception Analysis

Wallpaper* is one of the most prestigious design and luxury lifestyle publications globally. Being featured in May 2016 — in their **print magazine** — gave HEAVENSAKE exceptional early credibility with exactly the right audience: affluent, design-conscious, culturally sophisticated consumers who already drink Champagne at gallery openings and fashion shows.

The framing is unambiguously **luxury aspirational**: the brand is introduced as a champagne rival, not merely a sake product. Wallpaper* emphasises the visual design of the bottle almost as much as the liquid, which aligns perfectly with the brand's own positioning — that the full experience (design, events, aesthetics) is as important as the drink itself.

The article's title — "Rice as Nice" — is witty and affirming: the pun signals that sake can be elevated to the same cultural tier as champagne.
