# Review: Heavensake Junmai Ginjo and Junmai Daiginjo

**Publication:** Drinkhacker ("The Insider's Guide to Good Drinking")  
**Date:** October 14, 2019  
**Author:** Stephen Allison  
**URL:** https://www.drinkhacker.com/2019/10/14/review-heavensake-junmai-ginjo-and-junmai-daiginjo/

## Article Summary

An independent product review from Drinkhacker, a respected drinks media site. The reviewer tasted three HEAVENSAKE expressions at A16 restaurant in San Francisco and published letter grades with full tasting notes. The Junmai Daiginjo received a rare "A" grade — an outstanding result for any product reviewed at Drinkhacker.

---

## Key Brand Description by Drinkhacker

> *"Founded by Piper-Heidsieck cellar master, Regis Camus, Heavensake fuses Champagne's art of blending, and penchant for marketing flair, with the Japanese craft of sake brewing."*

> *"The result aims to solve one of sake's biggest hurdles in the western market: the problem of the daunting label, adorned with indiscernible Kanji, that is more intimidating than inviting."*

> *"Heavensake eschews this for a simple, romanized (and horrible all caps) label and a sleek bottle tailor-made for nightclubs and Instagram."*

> *"It's not traditional, but that's the point; Heavensake aims to position sake as a fresh, trendy luxury rather than a foreign curiosity."*

> *"If Austin's Texas Sake is your local craft brewery's new experiment, and Oakland's Den Sake is a bottle of small-batch, single-vineyard Pinot Noir, then Heavensake is Cristal."*

> *"We are happy to report that although the marketing and blending may take direction from France, Heavensake's flavor is decidedly Japanese."*

> *"I think the experiment works."*

## Tasting Notes & Ratings

### Heavensake Junmai Daiginjo (brewed by Dassai)
- **Rating: A** / $106
- **Nose:** Ripe pear, honeydew melon, subtle floral — "sweet and inviting"
- **Palate:** Impeccably balanced. Not too light or heavy, not acidic or savory. Long, slightly sweet, plush, rounded finish — "the flavors seem to intensify as the finish persists"
- **ABV:** 16%
- **Reviewer:** "It's terrific, but spendy."

### Heavensake Junmai Ginjo (brewed by Urakasumi)
- **Rating: B+** / $48
- **Nose:** Sweeter tropical fruit, bubblegum-like candied quality
- **Palate:** Acidic leaning, bright attack, crisp finish; more savory than the Daiginjo
- **Best for:** Food pairing
- **ABV:** 15%

### Heavensake Junmai 12 (brewed by Konishi)
- **Rating: Not recommended**
- **Nose:** Savory and sweet, like maple syrup
- **Palate:** Light sake (12% ABV), lacking structure due to dilution
- **Reviewer:** "I recommend sticking with the other two bottlings."

## Production Details (from article)

- **Junmai Daiginjo:** Blend of Dassai 23, Dassai 39, and two proprietary sakes
- **Junmai Ginjo:** Blend of three rice varieties (parallel to Champagne's Pinot Noir/Chardonnay/Meunier approach)

## Brand Perception Analysis

Drinkhacker's review is notably candid — the reviewer pushes back on the all-caps branding as "horrible" — but ultimately endorses the project with an "A" grade for the flagship expression. The **Cristal comparison** is the most quoted line: it tells HEAVENSAKE buyers exactly where the brand sits in the spirits hierarchy — at the very top of its category, for those who want the best and are willing to pay for it.

The reviewer's observation that "the flavor is decidedly Japanese" despite French direction is significant: it reassures sake purists that HEAVENSAKE hasn't compromised authenticity for accessibility. The conclusion "the experiment works" is understated but unambiguous approval.

The review's honesty about the Junmai 12's weaknesses also adds credibility — this is genuine editorial assessment, not puff coverage.
