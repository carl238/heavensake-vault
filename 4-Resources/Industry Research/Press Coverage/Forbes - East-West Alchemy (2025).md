# When Sake Meets Champagne: HEAVENSAKE's East–West Alchemy

**Publication:** Forbes  
**Date:** August 28, 2025  
**Author:** Brad Japhe  
**URL:** https://www.forbes.com/sites/bradjaphe/2025/08/28/when-sake-meets-champagne-heavensakes-eastwest-alchemy/

---

## Article Summary

A major Forbes feature profiling HEAVENSAKE's near-decade history and the launch of its most exclusive expression, the Noguchi Prestige. The article is structured as a conversation with Carl Hirschmann and Régis Camus, conducted by Forbes to mark the Noguchi Prestige launch (1,000 bottles globally). It positions HEAVENSAKE as a pioneer of assemblage in sake — and frames the brand as now "reaching new heights."

---

## Key Brand Description by Forbes

> *"Heavensake, led by Carl Hirschmann and Champagne master Régis Camus, innovates traditional sake by applying assemblage blending techniques."*

> *"Almost ten years later, the brand has celebrated numerous remarkable achievements in its journey: a selection of top-rated junmai bottles, placements in prestigious omakase restaurants worldwide, and creative initiatives in markets where sake previously lacked a presence."*

---

## Key Quotes

### Carl Hirschmann

> *"The initial inspiration came from the fact that all the founders of Heavensake were French. We recognized that the cultural ties and mutual admiration between Japan and France could create a compelling narrative, open new avenues, and facilitate intriguing exchanges."*

> *"There are multiple facets to consider: the tradition, the commitment to artistry, and the technical intricacies. Both processes are engineered in ways that are truly captivating. The creative journey begins with selecting the brewery. Each brewery possesses a distinctive taste profile and identity, which in turn influences the flavor and aroma we aim to develop. The Heavensake identity adds another layer: a floral bouquet, a fruity palate, a smooth mouthfeel, and a lingering finish. Ultimately, it is Régis's expertise that unites these elements into a cohesive composition, much like orchestrating a symphony where each part contributes to a harmonious whole."*

> *"The aroma is crucial, which is why we encourage people to use wine glasses for tasting. In Japan, even the finest sake is often consumed from ochoko porcelain cups. While this can be a lovely experience, it tends to diminish the olfactory aspect."*

> *"We tend to avoid the phrase 'tradition and innovation' since it's become cliché. While assemblage is indeed an age-old technique, applying it to sake is somewhat innovative, though we don't emphasize that term. We take pride in being the pioneers of this approach and seeing other breweries adopt blending and label it as such. The true essence lies in Régis's artistry, which resonates deeply with the tojis and embodies the soul of Heavensake."*

### Régis Camus

> *"The first step was always to clarify the style of Heavensake. After tasting numerous sakes, we concluded that our offerings would exclusively feature Junmai—no added spirits—with a focus on floral and fruity notes, freshness, length, minerality, and a silky texture—never a harsh alcohol burn. A key principle of blending is that the whole is greater than the sum of its parts."*

> *"As cellar master, I've traveled to Japan about twenty times. The country captivates me with its culture and cuisine, and I admire the Japanese dedication to their craft."*

> *"I've had countless memorable experiences through our various collaborations, but meeting Noguchi-san stands out. Despite our different cultural backgrounds, we immediately connected and respected one another."*

## Product Featured: Noguchi Prestige

- **Brewer:** Noguchi Naohiko, 92 years old — "Japan's foremost sake brewer"
- **Rice:** Yamadanishiki, Omachi, Miyamanishiki
- **ABV:** 37-proof (18.5%)
- **Tasting notes:** Slate and rose petal (aroma); tropical fruits, marzipan, deeper umami tones (palate)
- **Packaging:** Opaque white decanters, handcrafted by Arita Porcelain Lab
- **Edition:** Limited to 1,000 bottles globally
- **Pairing:** Rich raw fish, served slightly chilled in wine glass

## Brand Perception Analysis

Forbes treats HEAVENSAKE as a **legitimate luxury spirits brand** worthy of a full feature interview — not merely a trend piece. The framing is respectful and substantive: the assemblage concept is taken seriously, the brewers are named and their legacy cited, and the Noguchi collaboration is presented as a historic event. The brand is positioned as a **bridge between two world-class craft traditions**, with Camus's Champagne credentials lending Western credibility and Noguchi's legendary status lending Japanese authenticity.

The article's headline ("East–West Alchemy") perfectly encapsulates how Forbes sees HEAVENSAKE: a fusion of opposites elevated into something greater.
