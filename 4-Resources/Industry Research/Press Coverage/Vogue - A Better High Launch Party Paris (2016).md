## Article Summary

Vogue's coverage of HEAVENSAKE's inaugural launch event at Paris Fashion Week in March 2016 — the "A Better High" series, No. 1. The article/slideshow features photographs of celebrity attendees including Virgil Abloh, Raf Simons, Kelly Rowland, and Anja Rubik. This is one of the most culturally significant early press moments for the brand: being covered by Vogue at Paris Fashion Week, with fashion's most influential creative figures in attendance, positioned HEAVENSAKE at the epicentre of the global luxury fashion world from day one.

## Brand Perception Analysis

Vogue's coverage of the HEAVENSAKE launch is one of the most important single press moments in the brand's history. At a global publication with 25+ million readers, being featured in the context of Paris Fashion Week alongside Virgil Abloh and Raf Simons (two of fashion's most influential figures of the 2010s) placed HEAVENSAKE immediately in the **top tier of cultural luxury** — before most people had ever tasted it.

This coverage established several key brand associations that persist today:
1. **Fashion-world credibility** — not just a drinks brand, but a cultural object
2. **Paris as home base** — the brand's French-Japanese identity confirmed through its Paris Fashion Week positioning
3. **Celebrity adjacency** — serious fashion and music figures attending, not just influencers
4. **The "Better High" concept validated** — Vogue's coverage treats the events series as culturally newsworthy

The brand leveraged this Vogue moment to establish itself as a cultural brand first, a drinks brand second — a positioning strategy that continues to define HEAVENSAKE.
