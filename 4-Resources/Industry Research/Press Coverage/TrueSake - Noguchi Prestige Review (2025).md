## Tasting Notes (TrueSake)

**Nose:** Soothing minerality, complex & rich, yellow peach, mango, iris  
**Flavor:** Exotic fruits, almond financiers, passion fruit, acacia honey, pineapple, kiwi  
**Serve:** Chilled in a wine glass, store upright in a fridge

> *"The world-renowned Champagne cellar master (8× IWC Winner) was the first to bring assemblage to sake with the intention of delivering a touch of French joie de vivre to Japan. A virtuoso in flavor and fragrance, Régis believes sake is the drink of the future."*

## About Noguchi Naohiko (as described by TrueSake)

> *"'The God of Sake' spent over 70 years creating sake with one goal: making people happy. Widely recognized for his role in both the revival of the yamahai method and the 1980s ginjo boom, Noguchi-san continues to inspire generations of sake brewers."*

## Brand Perception Analysis

TrueSake's endorsement carries exceptional weight because their customers are the most educated sake buyers in America. The "Baller Sake" designation is TrueSake's own voice — irreverent, confident, and unmistakably enthusiastic. This is not a paid feature but an editorial review from a retailer that stocks hundreds of sakes and is selective with superlatives.

The fact that TrueSake emphasizes the assemblage technique as unlocking "unique flavors" — and quotes Noguchi-san's own astonishment — validates HEAVENSAKE's core brand claim at the highest level of the sake trade. This is **endorsement from the industry's most credible specialist voice** in the US market.

Also notable: TrueSake sells HEAVENSAKE's Junmai 12 (at $28.80), confirming ongoing retail relationship with the brand across its range, not just for the prestige expression.
