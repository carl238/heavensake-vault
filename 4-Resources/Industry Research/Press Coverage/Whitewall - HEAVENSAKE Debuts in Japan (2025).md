# HeavenSake Debuts in Japan: Fusing Japanese Heritage with French Artistry in One Sake Bottle

**Publication:** Whitewall (art, design and luxury lifestyle magazine)  
**Date:** January 7, 2025  
**Author:** Laurent Moïsi  
**URL:** https://whitewall.art/lifestyle/heavensake-debuts-in-japan-fusing-japanese-heritage-with-french-artistry-in-one-sake-bottle/

---

## Article Summary

Whitewall — an art and luxury lifestyle publication — covers HEAVENSAKE's landmark launch into the Japanese domestic market in November 2024, with distribution partner Imadeya. The interview with Carl Hirschmann reveals the brand's philosophical approach to entering Japan: with humility, restraint, and a deep respect for Japanese aesthetic values. The article marks one of the most significant milestones in the brand's history — bringing a French-blended sake brand back to the birthplace of sake itself.

---

## Key Brand Description by Whitewall

> *"This past November, HeavenSake launched in Japan, marking a defining moment for the brand, bringing its unique fusion of French assemblage and Japanese craftsmanship to the heart of sake culture."*

> *"With exclusive partnerships and immersive events, the launch not only celebrated the artistry of Junmai sake but also solidified HeavenSake's position as a bridge between tradition and innovation."*

> *"HeavenSake represents the future of sake — a chapter of playfulness, joie de vivre, and desirability."*

---

## Key Quotes: Carl Hirschmann

> *"We realized we were bringing something more philosophical to sake — a touch of French joie de vivre."*

> *"Japanese culture is inherently understated and elegant, and entering Japan, we wanted to approach it with that same humility."*

> *"I believe that any iconic alcohol brand needs to present a unique and powerful bottle design."*

---

## Japan Launch Details

- **Market context:** Japanese sake industry facing declining domestic sales; highly protected and traditional market
- **Distribution partner:** Imadeya (premium specialty sake distributor)
- **Events:** Sushi pairing dinner; intimate tasting sessions; pairing dinners at Sushi M
- **Approach:** Deliberate restraint — "no bold French gestures"
- **Reception:** Positive from Japanese audience and restaurant partners

---

## Brand Philosophy Insights (from article)

- Assemblage technique is now acknowledged by Japanese industry leaders as what the sake category needs
- HEAVENSAKE blends French joie de vivre (expressive, celebratory) with Japanese Ikiru Yorokobi (serene, internal joy)
- Encouraging use of wine glasses over ochoko cups to amplify aromas
- After 8 years of building globally, now entering sake's birthplace

---

## Brand Perception Analysis

Whitewall's framing of the Japan launch is richly symbolic: a French-Japanese brand returning sake to its homeland with a new perspective. The publication treats this as a design and cultural story — consistent with Whitewall's art world readership.

The most significant perception point here is **how HEAVENSAKE approaches Japan**: with humility, not triumphalism. Hirschmann's quote about not wanting to make "bold French gestures" signals cultural intelligence that resonates strongly with luxury and art world audiences who understand the importance of cultural sensitivity in luxury brand expansion.

The Imadeya partnership — Japan's most respected specialist sake and wine distributor — serves as validation from the Japanese trade itself. Whitewall's coverage amplifies this credibility for international readers.
