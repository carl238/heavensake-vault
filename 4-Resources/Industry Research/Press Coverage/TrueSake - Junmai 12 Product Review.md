## Tasting Notes (TrueSake)

**Nose:** Very gentle — hay, butterscotch, nougat, steamed rice  
**Palate:** Smooth, light, compact; elevated acidity; astringent complexity; persimmon, apricot, chicory, dark chocolate, caramel  
**Finish:** Tickle of bitterness; drying finish; more ricey expressions as it warms in the glass  
**WORD:** Ricey  
**Wine equivalent:** Tannin Reds / Crisp Whites  
**Beer equivalent:** Light Ales  
**Food pairing:** Meats, game, fowl, fish; complex pastas; crisp veggies

## Brand Perception Analysis

TrueSake's framing of Junmai 12 reveals how the brand's entry-level expression is perceived by specialist retailers: as a thoughtful, intentionally designed product that succeeds at its mission of accessibility without sacrificing craft. The comparison to red wine tannin lovers and craft coffee aficionados suggests TrueSake sees HEAVENSAKE as a brand that builds bridges between sake and other sophisticated beverage categories — exactly the positioning HEAVENSAKE aims for.

The low ABV (12%) at a $28.80 price point is noted without criticism; TrueSake presents this as a deliberate design choice rather than a compromise.
