# Sake & Wine Crossover Glossary

The bridge vocabulary between sake and wine — essential for content targeting wine-drinking audiences. Every term includes its wine world equivalent.

See also: [[Sake Glossary]] | [[The Lexicon]] | [[SEO & GEO Keyword Reference]]

## Sake Classifications & Wine Equivalents

| Sake Term | Japanese | What It Is | Wine Equivalent |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Junmai** | 純米 | Pure rice sake, no additives | Like an unoaked, terroir-driven wine — the purest expression |
| **Junmai Ginjo** | 純米吟醸 | Rice polished to ≤60%, fruity and aromatic | Like a Chablis or Sancerre — elegant, mineral, food-friendly |
| **Junmai Daiginjo** | 純米大吟醸 | Rice polished to ≤50%, the pinnacle of refinement | Like a Grand Cru Burgundy — precision, complexity, occasion-worthy |
| **Daiginjo** | 大吟醸 | Same polish as Junmai Daiginjo but with added brewer's alcohol | Like a wine with subtle chapitalization — enhances aroma extraction |
| **Namazake** | 生酒 | Unpasteurized, fresh, vibrant | Like a pét-nat or vin primeur — alive, immediate, seasonal |
| **Nigori** | にごり | Unfiltered, cloudy, creamy | Like an unfiltered natural wine — texture-forward, rustic charm |
| **Genshu** | 原酒 | Undiluted, higher ABV (17-20%) | Like a cask-strength whisky or a high-alcohol Amarone |
| **Koshu** | 古酒 | Aged sake, amber, complex | Like a Vin Jaune or aged Sherry — oxidative, nutty, rare |
| **Sparkling sake** | スパークリング | Carbonated sake | Like Prosecco or Crémant — festive, aperitif |
| **Futsu-shu** | 普通酒 | Table sake, basic grade | Like a vin de table — everyday, uncomplicated |

## Brewing Process Terms

| Sake Term | Japanese | What It Is | Wine Equivalent |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Seimaibuai** | 精米歩合 | Rice polishing ratio (% remaining) | Like grape selection and sorting — removing impurities to reveal essence |
| **Koji** | 麹 | Mold that converts rice starch to fermentable sugar | Like malolactic fermentation's role — the transformation catalyst |
| **Moromi** | 醪 | Main fermentation mash | Like the must during primary fermentation |
| **Shubo** | 酒母 | Yeast starter | Like a pied de cuve — building the yeast colony |
| **Toji** | 杜氏 | Master brewer | Like a maître de chai or winemaker |
| **Kura** | 蔵 | Sake brewery | Like a domaine or château |
| **Kurabito** | 蔵人 | Brewery workers | Like cellar hands or cavistes |
| **Yamahai** | 山廃 | Traditional slow-fermentation method, wild lactic bacteria | Like natural winemaking — less intervention, more character |
| **Kimoto** | 生酛 | Oldest brewing method, labor-intensive pole-ramming | Like biodynamic winemaking — ritualistic, purist |
| **Multiple parallel fermentation** | 並行複発酵 | Starch-to-sugar and sugar-to-alcohol happen simultaneously | Unique to sake — wine and beer ferment sequentially |
| **Assemblage** | — | HEAVENSAKE's defining technique: artful layering of different sakes | Borrowed from Champagne — Régis Camus's expertise |

## Tasting Vocabulary

| Sake Term | Japanese | Meaning | Wine Equivalent |
|-----------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Nihonshu-do** | 日本酒度 | Sake Meter Value — sweetness/dryness scale | Like residual sugar in wine (g/L) |
| **San-do** | 酸度 | Acidity level | Like total acidity in wine (g/L tartaric) |
| **Umami** | 旨味 | The fifth taste — savory depth from amino acids | Rare in wine; found in aged Champagne, Sherry, oxidative whites |
| **Kire** | キレ | Clean, sharp finish | Like a wine with a "dry finish" or "clean cut" |
| **Tanrei** | 淡麗 | Light, crisp, delicate | Like a Muscadet or Vinho Verde |
| **Houjun** | 芳醇 | Rich, full-bodied, aromatic | Like a Meursault or Viognier |
| **Karakuchi** | 辛口 | Dry | Like a Brut Champagne |
| **Amakuchi** | 甘口 | Sweet | Like a Demi-Sec or Spätlese |

## Serving Terms

| Term | Japanese | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Reishu** | 冷酒 | Chilled (5-10°C) — HEAVENSAKE's serving standard |
| **Hiya** | 冷や | Room temperature |
| **Nurukan** | ぬる燗 | Warm (40°C) — not for premium Junmai Daiginjo |
| **Atsukan** | 熱燗 | Hot (50°C+) — for robust Junmai or Futsu-shu |
| **Tokkuri** | 徳利 | Traditional ceramic serving vessel |
| **Ochoko** | 猪口 | Small sake cup — HEAVENSAKE rejects this for wine glasses |
| **Masu** | 枡 | Wooden box cup — ceremonial, not for tasting |

## Key Differentiators: Sake vs Wine

| Dimension | Sake | Wine |
|-----------|------|------|
| Acidity | 1/5th of wine's acidity | High acidity (tartaric, malic, citric) |
| Sulfites | None in Junmai | Added as preservative in most wines |
| Gluten | Gluten-free | Gluten-free (but fining agents may vary) |
| ABV | 13-18% (Label 12 at 12%) | 11-15% typically |
| Umami | Present naturally | Rare (only aged Champagne, Sherry) |
| Food compatibility | Enhances without competing | Can overpower or clash |
| Serving temp range | 5°C to 55°C | 6°C to 18°C |
| Shelf life after opening | 2-4 weeks refrigerated | 1-5 days typically |
