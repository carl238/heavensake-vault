# Brewery Partnerships

HEAVENSAKE's products are built on partnerships with Japan's most respected breweries. These relationships are strategic assets — they provide product authenticity, supply security, and cultural credibility. Managing them requires cultural sensitivity, clear communication, and operational discipline.

## Price Increase Context (Industry-Wide)

Both Shichiken (20%) and Niizawa (24%) are requesting price increases driven by rice cost increases — this is an industry-wide pressure on Japanese sake production. HEAVENSAKE must:

1. Analyze the actual margin impact on each product at each price increase level
2. Determine which increases can be absorbed vs. passed to customers
3. Negotiate selectively — full compliance on operations, negotiation on price
4. Explore alternative brewery partnerships to maintain pricing flexibility (Fukuju, Niigata, Fukuoka options being reviewed)

## Brewery Communication Protocol

**Front-facing contact:** Nana Zomen — all routine operational matters
**Escalation:** Sebastien L'Hermitte — pricing, commercial, strategic issues
**Carl involvement:** Relationship-critical matters only (Niizawa crisis; major decisions)
**Zak Gross:** Sample logistics, US market needs, distributor-brewery interface

**Cultural principle:** Japanese business relationships require consistency, respect for hierarchy, clear communication, and never being put in a position of public embarrassment. When something goes wrong, the fix is private, fast, and face-saving.

**Communication efficiency note:** Standard communications with [[Shichiken]] ([[Nana]]) take ~3 back-and-forth emails. For HEAVENSAKE, the same information exchange currently takes ~10. This is a documented inefficiency being addressed through Nana's expanded front-facing role.
