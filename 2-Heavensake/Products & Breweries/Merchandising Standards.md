# Merchandising Standards

Every physical HEAVENSAKE object — from a T-shirt to a Daruma — is a brand touchpoint and a potential content opportunity. The standard: Would Carl wear/display/gift this at his house in Monaco?

> "The difference between you burning money on something stupid and you actually generating free marketing value is whether people are going to wear it or not. And whether they're going to wear it or not depends on how it looks but just as much on how it feels." (Call 1)

---

## T-Shirt Standards

### Weight: 180–190 GSM

This is the lower end of good quality. Carl dislikes thick streetwear-style fabric.

> "The lowest weight that you can possibly imagine but is still good." (Call 1)

### Production

- **European supplier (Alex/Italians)** and **Japanese supplier (Maxime)** — order samples simultaneously from both
- Silkscreen only for complex fine-line designs (hexagon, detailed graphics)
- **Embroidery is banned for fine-line designs** — the hexagon lines and wordmark are too thin and will look cheap. (Call 33)
- Production split: ~200–300 units Japan, ~200–300 Italy/Europe. Sweater versions alongside T-shirts.

### Design Rules

- **No center-chest logo placement** — a trend, not timeless. (Call 11)
- **Never put just a plain logo on a T-shirt** — "it's cheap. Just a logo like this on a T shirt, it's cheap." (Call 24)
- **Breathing space is essential** — the design must breathe on the garment
- **One design = one story** — every T-shirt design must be answerable: why this, why here, why now?

### 10th Anniversary Shirt Specifics

- Large back print with breathing space between "HEAVENSAKE" wordmark and the mountain/Torii gate design
- Dates integrated into the design's crevices — not placed beneath the design
- **Do NOT add "10 years" text** — let the design speak. (Calls 8, 11, 25)

### New Bottle Illustration Shirt

- HEAVENSAKE bottle on black or dark gray ("a bit grayish")
- Logo with hexagon upper area
- Kanji somewhere — not split across lines (Call 25)

## The White Sweater — Permanent SKU

This is settled. Never revisit this decision.

> "The white sweater will exist for as long as Evansake exists because it is proven, it is tested. People like it, it's cheapish, it's efficient, it's cool, it's on brand, it's simple. Everything it works." (Call 1)

Never replace sweaters with plain logo T-shirts:

> "You need the sweater vibe. Otherwise it's cheap. Just a logo like this on a T shirt, it's cheap." (Call 24)

## Sake Taru (Branded Barrel)

The branded sake taru is a proven on-premise brand visibility and credibility tool.

> "It gives you credibility as you stray, you walk in, you see it. Just need to make it beautiful." (Call 1)

### Specifications

- **Standard size:** 60×60 cm minimum. Smaller ones "are lame and you cannot really read Heaven Sake." (Call 1)
- **Design:** Black and white. Torii gate simplified and clean. (Calls 1, 13, 16)
- **Cost:** ~€200 per piece when shipped in existing containers. (Call 1)
- **Initial order:** 5 units for market evaluation, then scale to 50+ units for Tokyo flood strategy.

### Content Rule

Every taru installed in a restaurant needs a piece of Instagram content made around it. No taru without content. (Call 1)

### Target Accounts

Restaurants currently displaying **Dassai taru** — HEAVENSAKE competes directly for that shelf/floor space. (Call 1)

### Custom Taru for Michelin-Star Accounts

Restaurant logo printed on a smaller taru + content creation about the relationship. This is relationship marketing + content creation simultaneously. (Call 1)

## The Daruma as Art Object

The 10th anniversary Daruma is the brand's artistic centerpiece — not just a prop.

### Specifications

- **Size:** 75cm high (70–76cm range)
- **Base:** White/unpainted
- **Surface:** HEAVENSAKE historical photos printed on washi paper, adhered to the surface; Mami San then paints the second eye and transforms the overall shell
- **Eye detail:** Brushstroke shape, slightly rectangular — not a perfect circle. (Call 1)
- **Face:** Traditional Japanese phoenix and dragon design (not modern cute/minimal or heron variants). "That's the traditional way of doing the dragon and the phoenix." (Call 2)

### The Phoenix is Personal

> "I prefer the phoenix because I have a personal relationship with the phoenix." (Call 8)

This is personal mythology connected to brand iconography — not arbitrary decoration.

### Pre-Printed vs. Hand-Painted

- Base face (phoenix/dragon) and kanji arrive pre-printed
- [[Mami San]] paints only the second eye and the luck kanji on the back (Calls 2, 8)
- For the 10th anniversary Daruma: she then paints over/around the washi paper photos

### Important: The Daruma is KEPT

**The Daruma is kept after completion — not burned.** This is culturally important and specific to HEAVENSAKE's use of the tradition. (Call 26)

## Materials Quality Standard

**No polyester or synthetic materials — ever.**

> "I don't want polyester anyway." (Call 33)

- Scrolls: Kyoto silk acceptable; Harajuku polyester rejected
- Merchandise: Natural fibers only
- Packaging: Washi paper, wood, silk — never plastic or synthetic substitutes
- Budget: 80,000 yen + calligraphy fee for high-quality scrolls (Call 33)
