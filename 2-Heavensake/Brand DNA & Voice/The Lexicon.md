# HEAVENSAKE SAKE GLOSSARY & BRAND LEXICON

*Master reference for HEAVENSAKE brand terminology and vocabulary**

---

## I. Brand Philosophy & Strategy

**Assemblage**
The defining artisanal technique of HEAVENSAKE. Borrowed from high-end French Champagne-making and introduced to sake by cellar master Régis Camus, it is the artistic layering of different sakes to create a complex, harmonious "third color" (like mixing blue and yellow to create purple). → See [[Assemblage — The Art & Science]]

**Blending**
The traditional, industrial counterpart to Assemblage. In HEAVENSAKE's vocabulary, "blending" is rejected as a term, as it implies mixing batches purely for taste uniformity, volume management, or cost-saving.

**Reviving (Not Redefining)**
The core mission of the brand. HEAVENSAKE expressly forbids the use of the marketing cliché "redefining sake." Instead, the goal is to revive a dormant UNESCO cultural heritage—the "drink of the gods"—and make it culturally relevant for the modern luxury world.

**Pure Elevation**
The elegant, public-facing value proposition of the brand. It represents the physical purity of the liquid (sulfite and additive-free) and the spiritual and social lift (elevation of status and coolness) experienced when drinking it.

**A Better High**
The visceral, lifestyle-driven "internal engine" of the brand. Used primarily for nightlife and celebration contexts, it refers to the physiological benefit of drinking pure Junmai sake: a cleaner, more euphoric buzz without the heaviness of wine or the sugar crash of spirits.

**Meeting of Masters**
The foundational concept of Franco-Japanese cultural exchange. It represents the collaboration between French savoir-faire (Régis Camus) and the profound, centuries-old traditions of Japanese brewing (the Tojis).

**The 15-Year Drop**
A storytelling concept highlighting that the pristine water used to brew the sake takes 15 years to filter through the Japanese mountains before it is used. It is referred to as "eternity in a drop."

**Assemblage of Moments**
The thematic concept for HEAVENSAKE's 10-year anniversary, celebrating how the brand has brought sake into versatile, modern luxury settings — from fashion shows and art galleries to Michelin-starred dining and nightclub takeovers.

## II. The Ritual & Terminology

**The Wine Glass Ritual**
HEAVENSAKE's non-negotiable rule for consumption. The sake must be poured into a large wine glass to allow the complex floral and fruity aromas unlocked by assemblage to breathe and evolve.

**Ochoko**
The traditional, small cylindrical ceramic sake cup. HEAVENSAKE explicitly rejects the ochoko because its shape removes 30-40% of the aromatic experience and functionally limits the beverage to a "sushi counter" rather than an elevated dining context.

**Junmai**
Pure rice sake with no additives, no added sugar, gluten-free, and sulfite-free. HEAVENSAKE exclusively produces Junmai sake.

**Toji**
A traditional Japanese sake brewmaster. The masters who collaborate with Régis Camus to produce the base liquids.

## III. Symbols & Aesthetics

**The Torii Gate**
A core visual symbol used in the brand's merchandising and 10-year anniversary assets. It represents the passage from the mundane, everyday reality into the elevated "Heaven" of the brand experience.

**The Daruma**
A traditional Japanese doll symbolizing perseverance. Used by HEAVENSAKE for its 10-year anniversary ritual, where the act of painting the doll's eyes represents celebrating past achievements and looking toward a new chapter.

**The Hexagon**
A graphic element integrated into the HEAVENSAKE logo, representing the structured, French heritage (L'Hexagone) meeting Japanese artistry.

**Pink Resonance**
The creative concept driving the [[Sakura Prestige]] / Hanami launch, symbolizing the mixture of red warmth and white clarity to evoke spring, rebirth, and fleeting elegance.

## IV. Products & Collaborations

**Prestige I (Noguchi)**
The ultra-premium expression created in collaboration with the legendary Naohiko Noguchi, widely revered as the "God of Sake." Vintage assemblage 2018-2020. Rice: Yamada Nishiki, Omachi, Miyamanishiki. ABV 18.5%. Nose: yellow peach, mango, iris. Flavor: exotic fruits, almond financiers, passion fruit, acacia honey, pineapple, kiwi.

**Prestige II (Niizawa)**
A high-end expression created in collaboration with the Niizawa Brewery. Junmai Daiginjo. Rice: Yamada Nishiki, Omachi, Miyamanishiki. ABV 15%. Nose: spring flowers, William pear floral, subtle minerality. Flavor: cherimoya, lychee, kiwi.

**Sakura / Hanami Prestige**
A limited-edition release of Prestige II housed in a unique "sanded pink" matte ceramic bottle, launched to celebrate spring, cherry blossoms, and the women behind the brand.

**Label Azur (Junmai Ginjo)**
Housed in a blue bottle, created in collaboration with the Dewazakura brewery. Rice: Dewasansan, Tsuyahime. ABV 15.5%. Nose: spring-like freshness, green apple, mint. Pairs well with: Jamon Iberico, Niçoise Salad, Seafood.

**Label Noir (Junmai Daiginjo)**
The flagship ultra-premium expression. Created with Niizawa Brewery. Rice: Yamada Nishiki, Dewasansan, Yukimegami. ABV 15%. Nose: cantaloupe, tangerine, lychee. Flavor: refreshing acidity, minerality, ripe fruit. Pairs well with: Uni, Asparagus, Caviar, Tomato Bruschetta.

**Label Orange (Junmai Daiginjo)**
Created with Urakasumi. Rice: Yamada Nishiki, Kura no hana. ABV 16.3%. Nose: white lily, pineapple, lychee, melon. Pairs well with: white fish, white truffle risotto, red meat, creamy cheeses.

**Junmai 12**
Accessible entry expression with Konishi Brewery. 12% ABV. Rice: Nihonbare. Nose: salted caramel, kumquat, almond, cacao. Pairs well with: seafood, risotto, dark chocolate.

**Arita Porcelain Lab**
The historic Japanese artisan studio that handcrafts the sculptural ceramic vessels for the Prestige lines, blending vintage glaze techniques with modern design.

## VI. Core Brand Concepts (Extended)

**Junmai**
A classification of sake made with only four ingredients: rice, water, yeast, and koji. HEAVENSAKE exclusively produces Junmai to maintain the highest standards of purity.

**Mineral Perfection**
The result of the natural 15-year filtration process of water through Japan's mountain peaks, which serves as the base for all HEAVENSAKE expressions.

**Pure by Nature, Elevated by Art**
The official brand mantra. It summarizes the union of raw Japanese natural ingredients (Purity) and French blending expertise (Art).

**The Content Machine**
The 2026 strategic shift toward high-volume, AI-assisted, yet brand-aligned digital output designed to maintain a "magazine-vibe" rhythm across social channels.

**Hanami (The Season of Rebirth)**
A critical marketing window in Spring (March/April) centered on the Pink Prestige launch and the theme of renewal.

**Master of Assemblage**
The title held by Régis Camus — the world's most-awarded champagne maker (8x IWC Sparkling Winemaker of the Year, a record that has never been matched). He applies his lifetime of champagne expertise to the creation of HEAVENSAKE. Note: Do not refer to him as "Cellar Master" — his correct current title is "world's most-awarded champagne maker" and "HEAVENSAKE Master of Assemblage."

## VIII. Sake Craft Glossary (Poetic Edition)

**Assemblage アッサンブラージュ** / ah-sahm-blahj /
*The composition. The third color.*
The artistic act of composing multiple sake expressions into a single, elevated whole — creating a flavor that did not exist before. Born in the cellars of Champagne, brought to the world of sake by Régis Camus, HEAVENSAKE's chef de cave. Not to be confused with blending, which standardizes. Assemblage transcends. It is the difference between mixing blue and yellow to produce green — and discovering purple.

**Toji 杜氏** / toh-jee /
*Ancient knowledge, living and breathing.*
The master brewer. The soul of the kura. A Toji is not a technician — they are a guardian of tradition, carrying decades of sensory memory in their hands and palate.

**Kura 蔵** / koo-rah /
*A cathedral of fermentation.*
The sake brewery. Literally translated as 'storehouse,' a kura is where the alchemy begins — where rice, water, koji, and time conspire to create something extraordinary. HEAVENSAKE works exclusively with Japan's most revered kura, from Dassai to Urakasumi, Shichiken to Naohiko Noguchi's legendary institute.

**Junmai 純米** / joon-my /
*Nothing added. Nothing taken away.*
The purity benchmark. Junmai sake is brewed with only rice, water, koji, and yeast — no additives, no added alcohol, no compromises. All HEAVENSAKE expressions are Junmai.

**Daiginjo 大吟醸** / dai-gin-jo /
*Half the grain. Twice the grace.*
The pinnacle of the brewer's art. Sake made from rice polished to at least 50% of its original size. The result is a liquid of exceptional delicacy: luminous, floral, impossibly refined.

**Ginjo 吟醸** / gin-jo /
*The door left slightly open.*
One step below Daiginjo in rice milling. Polished to at least 60%, yielding expressive aromas and a bright, fruit-forward character. Approachable luxury that invites the first sip.

**Seimaibuai 精米歩合** / say-my-boo-eye /
*Subtract to elevate.*
The milling rate — the percentage of the rice grain remaining after polishing. A seimaibuai of 50% means half the grain has been removed. The lower the number, the more refined the sake.

**Koji 麹** / koh-jee /
*The first whisper of what's to come.*
The invisible architect of sake. Koji is a mold — Aspergillus oryzae — cultivated on steamed rice, its enzymes breaking starches into fermentable sugars. Without koji, there is no sake. A process so precise and alive that koji-making was designated an Intangible Cultural Heritage.

**Namazake 生酒** / nah-mah-zah-keh /
*Alive in the glass.*
Unpasteurized sake — raw and vital, alive with the energy of the kura. Fleeting by nature, it rewards those who seek it out and drink it now.

**Kanpai 乾杯** / kahn-pie /
*The ritual begins here.*
The Japanese toast — literally 'dry cup,' an expression of shared joy and intention. To say kanpai with a glass of HEAVENSAKE is to honor 2,000 years of brewing heritage.

**En Bouche** / ahn boosh /
*The finish that stays with you.*
A French wine term meaning 'in the mouth' — used at HEAVENSAKE to describe the full palate experience: texture, flavor, evolution, and most critically, length. Régis Camus will start an assemblage over entirely if the length is not right.

**Umami 旨味** / oo-mah-mee /
*The taste that makes the meal complete.*
The fifth taste — savory, deep, mouth-coating, singular. Sake's secret weapon at the table. At HEAVENSAKE, assemblage is used to balance and amplify umami without letting it dominate.

**Shokunin 職人** / shoh-koo-nin /
*A life's work in a single sip.*
The Japanese concept of the dedicated artisan — a craftsperson who pursues mastery as a lifelong commitment. HEAVENSAKE is built at the intersection of two shokunin cultures: the Japanese Toji and the French chef de cave.

**Wabi-Sabi 侘寂** / wah-bee sah-bee /
*Perfect in its imperfection.*
The Japanese aesthetic of finding beauty in imperfection and impermanence. Sake, by its nature, is wabi-sabi — seasonal, alive, never exactly the same twice.

**Ma 間** / mah /
*The silence that completes the music.*
The Japanese concept of negative space — the pause, the breath, the silence between notes. In sake, ma is the finish: the moment after the swallow when flavor lingers, evolves, and eventually releases. Régis Camus designs for ma.

**Monozukuri ものづくり** / moh-noh-zoo-koo-ree /
*Made with intention. Received with gratitude.*
The art and spirit of making things — the Japanese philosophy that elevates manufacturing into devotion. Every bottle of HEAVENSAKE is the product of monozukuri.

**Ikigai 生き甲斐** / ee-kee-gai /
*The reason everything began.*
The Japanese concept of purpose — the reason to rise in the morning. For the three French founders, it is the mission: to revive a drink of the gods and return it to the world stage.

## IX. Breweries & Sake Terminology

### The Breweries (The Masters)

**Naohiko Noguchi Sake Institute (Noguchi)**
The brewery led by Naohiko Noguchi, known as the "God of Sake." HEAVENSAKE collaborated with him to create the ultra-premium Prestige I, utilizing unreleased sake from his personal collection. Prefecture: Ishikawa.

**Niizawa Brewery**
The legendary brewery that partnered with HEAVENSAKE to produce Label Noir and Prestige II. Also the exclusive liquid provider for the limited-edition Sakura/Hanami Prestige pink bottle. Prefecture: Miyagi. Ranked #1 sake brewery for six consecutive years.

**Shichiken**
A brewery with centuries of savoir-faire from the Kitahara family, located where the pristine waters of Hakushu flow. Partners behind Label Azur (Junmai Ginjo). Founded 1750.

**Urakasumi**
Highly prominent and respected Japanese brewery, partners for Label Orange. Founded in 1724, provides the sacred sake for the Shiogama Shrine. The 13th generation of the Saura family manages this brewery. Saura-san is the founder of the Sake Samurai Association.

**Dewazakura**
Established in 1892, Yamagata Prefecture's Dewazakura sake brewery kick-started a national trend for Ginjo-style sake with the 1980 release of their signature Oka Ginjo. Partners for Label Azur.

**Konishi**
Located in the Hyogo Prefecture, Konishi is the oldest single-family brewery dating back to 1550. President Shintarō Konishi is the 15th generation managing the business. Partners for Junmai 12.

**Dassai**
The first brewery HEAVENSAKE collaborated with to prove the concept of Assemblage. NOTE: Not a current brewery partner.

## X. The HEAVENSAKE Taste Profile

**The House Style**
Envisioned by Régis Camus, the overarching HEAVENSAKE profile is characterized by a fruity palate, a floral nose, a silky mouthfeel, and an extended finish (long en bouche).

**Purity & Wellness Profile**
All expressions are pure Junmai, meaning they are 3 times less acidic than wine, gluten-free, sulfite-free, and contain no added sugar, offering a "mindful choice for modern indulgence."

**Label Azur (Junmai Ginjo)**
Crisp and floral with whispers of white peach and apricot. Perfectly paired with sushi, oysters, or linguine alle vongole.

**Label Noir (Junmai Daiginjo)**
Silky and elegant, featuring notes of pear, wild berries, and muscat grapes. Designed to pair with rich foods like Kobe beef, uni, lobster, or caviar.

**Prestige I (Noguchi)**
Described as a majestic, complex, and delicate symphony. Features a silky mouthfeel, perfect length, and vibrant layers of peaches, mangos, and iris.

## XI. Signature Events & Cultural Moments

**A Better High Events**
An annual, vibrant mini-festival hosted by HEAVENSAKE over the past eight years (prominently at the American Cathedral in Paris). An exclusive celebration where sake is the only alcohol served, designed to showcase its versatility to fashion, art, and music crowds.

**Hanami (Sakura Season)**
The culturally resonant Japanese cherry blossom viewing season (late March to April). HEAVENSAKE utilizes this season to launch its limited-edition pink Sakura Prestige bottle.

**International Sake Day (October 1st)**
A major global milestone for the brand, used as a key moment for strategic influencer gifting and brand awareness campaigns.
