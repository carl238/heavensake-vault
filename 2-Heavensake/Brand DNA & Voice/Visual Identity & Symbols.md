# Visual Identity & Symbols

The complete visual philosophy of HEAVENSAKE — the aesthetic north star, the key symbols, and the design rules that govern every image, bottle, and activation. All creative decisions must pass through "The Carl Filter."

---

## The Four Visual DNA Words

> "Light, elevation, purity, minimalism. Light. We've always been about light. It's always been about light." (Call 26)

These four words are the master filter for every visual decision. If a design contradicts any of them, it needs revision.

---

## The Aesthetic North Star

**"Stanley Kubrick meets Daft Punk"**

This is the definitive four-word brief for HEAVENSAKE's visual identity. It means:

- **Kubrick:** High-contrast. Precise. Minimal. Psychologically charged. Imagery that holds tension. Composition over chaos.
- **Daft Punk:** Futuristic. Mysterious. Masked. Cultural currency. Not mainstream — culturally plugged-in. Timeless cool without nostalgia.

Combined: **High-contrast minimalism with cultural heat.** The aesthetic feels expensive without announcing it. It is art, not advertising.

## The Three Key Symbols

### 1. The Torii Gate

The Torii gate is the brand's primary sacred symbol. In Japanese culture, a Torii marks the threshold between the mundane and the sacred — the passage from ordinary to divine. For HEAVENSAKE, it represents:

- The threshold between the physical world and transcendence ("Pure Elevation")
- The Franco-Japanese crossing — two cultures meeting at a gate
- The invitation: crossing into a different world of taste and experience

**IP note:** Carl flagged concern that the Torii gate design for Monaco must be legally protected. See [[Legal & Compliance]].

### 2. The Hexagon

A secondary symbol appearing in brand materials. The hexagon represents:

- Structure within nature (honeycomb — precision and harmony)
- The six sides mirroring expressions in the product range
- Geometric order meeting organic beauty — the same tension as Franco-Japanese Assemblage

**Hexagon restraint rule:** Use the hexagon with restraint — not on every post.

> "In general, I think I want to use the hexagon less." (Call 11)

> "Every post has that hexagon, it becomes too much." (Call 16)

In carousels, the hexagon creates visual rhythm only when used selectively. On Shikishi message cards: hexagon logo in UV acrylic print — proportions must be right. (Calls 25, 27)

### 3. The Bottle as "A Brush Full of Ink"

The HEAVENSAKE bottle is described internally as "a brush full of ink" — an object that carries potential creative expression. It is not a container; it is an instrument. This framing applies to product photography and storytelling:

- The bottle should always appear as an active object, not a passive prop
- Pouring, rotating, liquid-filled — never static on a white background
- The Arita ceramic Prestige bottle extends this concept: an art object as much as a vessel

## Logo & Wordmark Rules

- **The wordmark must always appear.** The hexagon alone is not enough: "There's no Heaven Sake anywhere. Where's Heaven Sake?" (Call 16) The brand recognition is still being built — every visual touchpoint must include the legible name.
- **No design decisions without Carl.** Particularly for the website. (Call 16)
- **Generally right-bound alignment** when text must choose left or right. (Call 11)
- **Brand elements placed high:** "You should always start putting it as high as possible because it is heaven." (Call 31)
- **Rule of thirds for product imagery:** Bottle centered at the intersection point; logo/wordmark in the upper portion; hexagon centered cleanly. (Call 33)
- **Cover photo horizon line:** Must bisect the negative space — not cut through or touch the hexagon. The hexagon floats cleanly above the horizon. (Call 32)
- **One line is always cleaner than two:** "Two lines is always too much. Like why two lines? One you see like one line." (Call 11)
- **"PURE ELEVATION" in ALL CAPS** for the tagline wherever used. (Call 33)

## The "Papa Aesthetics" Ban

The following visual directions are **BANNED** at HEAVENSAKE. This is called "papa aesthetics" — the aesthetic of a previous generation that communicates tradition as nostalgia rather than living heritage.

| Banned Element | Why It's Banned |
|---------------|----------------|
| Dusty calligraphy | Evokes souvenir shop, not cultural authority |
| Wax seals | Historical, not contemporary |
| Mount Fuji as wallpaper | Japanese cliché without a French twist |
| Zen platitudes on packaging | Belongs on a tea bag |
| Cherry blossoms without context | Only acceptable in the [[Sakura Prestige Campaign]] with the "Pink Resonance" framing |
| Ochoko (cylindrical ceramic cup) | Functionally banned — removes 30–40% of aroma; use Wine Glass only |

**The test:** Does this image make HEAVENSAKE look like a 1990s sake brand or a 2026 global cultural luxury brand? If the former — delete it.

## Color Philosophy

- The Brand Bible colors are not fixed canon: "It wasn't that precise. It was their suggestion... The Brand Bible needs a bit of love." (Call 11) Treat it as a base to refine, not law.
- **Label Noir = all black aesthetic:** The butterfly must be black. Tone-on-tone. No surprising accent colors breaking the noir theme. (Call 15)
- **Red is off-brand:** "Heaven Sake is not very red vibes." (Call 11) Red accents from the Rolling Stones logo were rejected.
- **Avoid "flat" colors:** Carl rejected a pink Prestige printing sample because "the pink is a little bit too flat." (Call 1)
- **Prestige 3 = matte sanded pink:** This informs all associated content's color direction. (Call 20)
- **Soft backgrounds:** "I think we need the background color to be a little bit more soft." (Call 9)

---

## Typography Rules

- **White text with black outlines** on certain print/social materials — the existing font gets an outline layer added, not a new font. (Call 7)
- **Kanji spacing:** Elements should be slightly closer together; avoid too much spacing. (Call 9)
- **Prestige 3 kanji:** A more modern simplified kanji was chosen — "I want Prestige 3 to be a bit more modern. The simple F looks absolutely amazing." (Call 8)
- **Center-chest logo placement is rejected as a trend**, not timeless. (Call 11)
- Clean, modern type only — sans-serif or razor-precision serif. No decorative script fonts.

## Business Cards

**ALL HEAVENSAKE business cards are vertical.** Non-negotiable.

> "All Evansaki business cards are vertical. You need to stick to the base design... It's a vertical with Heaven Sake [logo] at the top part." (Call 22)

- Japanese on the back, English on the front.
- Paper choice should match the quality reference established by Tsuruta-san. (Call 22)

## Feed Aesthetics (Instagram)

- **Instagram is the #1 brand window.** "The Instagram needs to be the perfect, perfect window." (Call 7) It is seen by tens of thousands; a physical object is seen by hundreds.
- **Cover/hero images** must be harmonized with the feed — background softness is essential. (Calls 7, 9)
- **Soft edges concept from website:** Straight angles at the bottom, soft edges at the top. (Call 9)
- **Do not post two carousels back to back.** (Call 16)
- **Balance product types:** "There's all these prestige content is a little bit intense — it's all prestige. So that's why the black bottle becomes really important to post." (Call 16)

## Thumbnail Thinking

Every video must be designed to stand out at 1/9th of a screen size, with zero sound, in a fraction of a second:

> "You don't even have two seconds. You have a quarter of a second in a ninth of the screen... you need something that if it's just light..." (Call 26)

## Arita Porcelain Lab — The Material Language of the Prestige Line

[[Matsumoto-san]] at [[Arita Porcelain Lab]] produces the sculptural ceramic vessels for the Prestige range using:
- **Mud casting** — hand-formed, not machine-pressed
- **Iron-infused glazes** — creates organic surface variation; no two bottles are identical
- **Hand-drilling** — each bottle is a unique piece
- **"Sanded pink" matte finish** — for the Sakura Prestige/Hanami Prestige; dipped head-first (upside down) into pink glaze, dried, then fired in kiln at 2,000–3,000°C. The process is applied by hand by a female artisan. (Calls 13, 14)

The Prestige bottle is explicitly framed as an **art object**, not just packaging. It belongs in a gallery as much as on a table.

## Materials Quality Standard

- **No polyester or synthetic materials** — for scrolls, merchandise, or art objects. "I don't want polyester anyway." Silk is acceptable. Authentic, natural materials only. (Call 33)
- **Scrolls:** Regional provenance matters — Kyoto (silk) acceptable; Harajuku (polyester) rejected. Budget: 80,000 yen + calligraphy fee. (Call 33)

## Cultural Reference Framework

Every visual must have a Franco-Japanese lens — Japanese references must carry a French twist, or they become cliché.

**Approved cultural juxtapositions:**
- Ink-brush tradition + minimalist European photography
- Ceramic artisanship + modernist architecture
- Japanese brewery ritual + Champagne cellar master formality
- Tokyo street culture + Monaco luxury setting
- Kinbaku (Shibari) art + French fashion film aesthetic

**Never:** Japanese visual culture in isolation (without the French gaze transforming it).
