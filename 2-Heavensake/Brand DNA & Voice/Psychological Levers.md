## Overview

A comprehensive psychological lever framework mapped to each HEAVENSAKE channel, built directly from the brand's strategic documents. Each lever works differently depending on where the audience is in their journey. The goal is to match the right emotional trigger to the channel's native context — not apply them uniformly.

## The Five Levers — In Depth

### Desire

The highest-frequency lever — it belongs on every channel but must be grounded in sensory experience, never generic aspiration. The copy guidelines are explicit: *evocative* beats *descriptive*.

- **On Instagram:** Desire is triggered by a visual hook before any brand truth.
- **On the product page:** The formula is "lead with desire, support with facts."
- **For the Social Host persona** (45–60, Hamptons): The desire trigger is object-level — the bottle as conversation starter, something guests will ask about.

Use [[The Lexicon]] seed phrases to activate this lever: *Silk on the tongue. Moonlight in liquid form.*

---

### Scarcity

Scarcity increases perceived value only when it feels true, not manufactured. The brand's own strategy frames this as: introduce limited edition releases or seasonal specials, making certain products available only at specific times or in limited quantities.

- **In email:** This means waitlist language for the VHNW Collector and Craft Connoisseur ("allocation priority, concierge updates"), not countdown timers.
- **On TikTok/Reels:** Scarcity is implied through energy and cultural heat — the sense that this moment is passing — rather than explicit "only X left" copy.

See [[Buyer Personas]] → The VHNW Collector and Champagne Switcher sections.

---

### Ritual

Ritual is the most underleveraged lever in the channel mix. The strategy documents flag *symbolism and ritual* as a key luxury brand tool — the hexagonal logo and assemblage concept should function as "symbols of Franco-Japanese heritage," not just design elements.

- **In email:** Ritual is built through the educational welcome flow: a 4-email sequence that teaches temperature, glassware, and occasion before asking for a purchase.
- **At events:** The tasting itself *is* the ritual — the sequence of presentation, the pour, the nose — and copy should frame the serving experience as ceremony, not instruction.

The [[Brand Voice Bible]] Non-Negotiable Pillar III: The Wine Glass is the foundation of this lever.

---

### Heritage

Heritage is the most credible authority signal HEAVENSAKE has — and the one most distinctly its own. The "Meeting of Masters" narrative (Régis Camus, 8× Best Champagne Winemaker, meets Dassai, Tatenokawa, Urakasumi) is the central myth.

- **On YouTube and long-form content:** Heritage is deployed as the *primary* lever — Régis Camus and the Japanese Tojis are treated as characters, not credentials.
- **For the Craft Connoisseur and Japanophile personas:** Heritage communicates "cultural authority," not nostalgia — readable luxury that respects tradition while delivering modern expression.

See [[Brand Story — The Assemblage]] and [[Narrative Pillars]] → Revival & Heritage.

---

### Exclusivity

Exclusivity must be structural, not just verbal — the strategy is explicit that the brand should never discount in copy, because "value is communicated through exclusivity, craft, and experience, never price reduction."

- **For the VHNW Collector segment:** Exclusivity is activated through *access* mechanics: direct outreach, private events, allocation lists, investment/provenance narrative.
- **On Instagram:** Exclusivity is implied by placement (select high-end venues, Michelin-starred chef endorsements) and by what is *not* shown — mass-market contexts, price callouts, or promotional framing.

See [[Buyer Personas]] → The VHNW Collector and [[Sake Glossary]] → Club Heaven.

## Channel Activation Notes

**Never apply levers uniformly.** A countdown timer works on TikTok; it kills trust with the VHNW Collector. "Only X left" copy drives the Champagne Switcher; it reads as desperate to the Craft Connoisseur.

**The Desire lever belongs everywhere** — but must be grounded in sensory specificity from [[The Lexicon]], never generic aspiration.

**The Exclusivity lever must be earned** — it requires genuine scarcity (limited production, allocation lists) or genuine access restriction (private events, direct-only communication). Fake exclusivity breaks trust permanently with the top-value personas.
