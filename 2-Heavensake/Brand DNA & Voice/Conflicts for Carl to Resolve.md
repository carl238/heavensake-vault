## 🚩 CONFLICT 1 — Régis Camus Title / Current Role

**This is the most sensitive conflict — it affects how we describe Régis in all brand communications.**

| | Version | Detail |
|-|---------|--------|
| **A** (Brand Knowledge Base) | "Cellar Master of Champagne Charles Heidsieck" | The Brand Knowledge Base calls him this in the Cultural Heritage section. |
| **B** (Régis Camus vault note — correct career timeline) | Retired from Piper-Heidsieck/Charles Heidsieck in 2018; retired from Rare Champagne in 2022. **Current role is purely HEAVENSAKE.** | The Régis Camus vault note contains his full, documented career timeline with citations from Wine Spectator and Forbes. |

**Assessment:** Version B (the vault note) is **factually correct**. Régis retired from champagne entirely in March 2022. Calling him "Cellar Master of Champagne Charles Heidsieck" in 2026 is inaccurate — he no longer holds that role.

**However:** His 50-year champagne career and 8 IWC titles remain the single most powerful proof point for HEAVENSAKE's credibility. The brand should reference his champagne **legacy**, not a current title he no longer holds.

**Recommended resolution:**
- In brand communications: "Régis Camus, the world's most-awarded champagne maker and HEAVENSAKE's Master Blender"
- Do NOT say "Cellar Master of Champagne Charles Heidsieck" — he left in 2018
- The vault Régis Camus note has the correct language — use that as the master reference

**Carl: Please confirm this is the correct framing for all future communications.**

**This affects the 10th anniversary claim and all "X years" communications.**

| | Version | Detail |

| **A** (Brand Knowledge Base) | "The brand was founded in 2016 and its 10th anniversary (2026) is a milestone moment across all communications." | The KB is explicit: founded 2016. |
| **B** (Multiple vault notes and press sources) | Some vault notes and press coverage (e.g., Forbes 2017 article titled "Thanksgiving Sake") suggest the brand's first public presence was 2016–2017. The Vogue article about the "Better High Launch Party Paris (2016)" supports 2016 as the founding year. However, some notes say 2017. | The vault is inconsistent internally. |

**Assessment:** The Brand Knowledge Base (most recent, compiled from 37 calls) is consistent: **2016 is the founding year.** The Paris launch event documented in Vogue also confirms 2016. The "10th anniversary in 2026" math supports 2016 as the founding year.

**However:** The shibari/Wallpaper article is dated 2017 ("Kinbaku Launch Event 2017"), which may indicate the brand had a 2016 soft launch in Paris and a 2017 wider launch. This would make both years partially correct depending on definition.

**Recommended resolution:**
- **Use 2016 as the official founding year** — this is what the Brand Knowledge Base states and what the 10th anniversary claim requires
- Acknowledge 2017 as the year of the first major product launches in some markets if needed for precision

**Carl: Please confirm the official founding year for all brand communications. The 10th anniversary narrative depends on this.**

**Assessment:** This is a significant discrepancy. €800/$800 for Prestige II seems high if it was previously at $300. Prestige I (Noguchi, the ultra-premium) at $700–800 is plausible. Prestige II at $800 would represent a major price increase.

- The Brand Knowledge Base is using European retail pricing (€800) while the vault note was using US retail pricing ($300 for Prestige II)

**Carl: Please confirm the current correct retail price for each Prestige bottle:**
- Prestige 1 (Noguchi): €___ / $___
- Prestige 2 (Niizawa): €___ / $___
- Prestige 3 (Sakura/Hanami): €___ / $___

**And confirm whether the Sakura Hanami bottle at 98,500 JPY (~$600–800) is the same as Prestige 3 or a separate product.**

## 🚩 CONFLICT 5 — Database / Customer List Size

**Affects CRM strategy, segmentation approach, and revenue projections.**

| | Version | Detail |
|-|---------|--------|
| **A** (Brand Knowledge Base — Dec 2025–Mar 2026 calls) | ~3,000 email addresses total; ~315 purchased customers; **top 20** (not top 20%) customers as the priority focus | The KB is specific: 3,000 emails, 315 buyers, focus on the top 20 absolute people. |
| **B** (Existing vault notes — earlier data) | ~5,000 total email contacts across ClubHeaven + broader network; ~600–689 Shopify records; ~60–62 identified "whale" buyers (top 20%) | Substantially different numbers, particularly the 600–689 Shopify records vs. 315 purchased customers. |

**Assessment:** These numbers may both be correct if measuring different things:
- 315 = actual paying customers (people who completed a purchase)
- 600–689 = total Shopify records including abandoned carts, accounts with no purchases, test accounts, etc.
- 3,000 emails = full mailing list including non-customers
- 5,000 emails = broader network including event contacts, ClubHeaven signups

**However:** The shift from "top 60–62 whales (top 20%)" to "top 20 people (absolute)" is a meaningful strategic difference.

**Carl: Please confirm:**
1. What is the correct total mailing list size? (3,000 or 5,000?)
2. What is the correct number of purchasing customers? (315 or 600–689?)
3. Is the focus "top 20 people" (absolute) or "top 20%" of buyers?
