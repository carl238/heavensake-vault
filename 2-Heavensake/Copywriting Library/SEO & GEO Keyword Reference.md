## IV. Wine-Crossover Terms

These terms capture the wine-to-sake switcher audience — one of HEAVENSAKE's primary buyer personas.

| Term | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|
| Sake for wine lovers | Direct audience bridge — the Champagne Switcher and Social Host personas |
| Sake instead of white wine | Positioning play — sake as a wine alternative, not a separate category |
| Natural wine alternative | Sulfite-free, gluten-free, no additives — cleaner than natural wine |
| Clean alcohol / no hangover alcohol | The "Better High" narrative — purity = better morning after |
| Sake tasting notes | Wine vocabulary applied to sake — familiar language for crossover audience |
| Food-friendly sake | Sake doesn't fight with food — lower acidity than wine, natural umami |
| Sake like champagne | Assemblage technique borrowed from champagne — Régis Camus connection |

## V. Reddit & Community Language

Terms used in r/sake, r/japanese, and sake enthusiast communities. Important for authentic content.

| Term | Meaning | HEAVENSAKE Relevance |
|------|---------|---------------------|
| SMV (Sake Meter Value) | Sweetness/dryness scale. Negative = sweeter, positive = drier | Products range from -2.4 (Noir) to +6 (Prestige I) |
| Seimaibuai | Rice polishing ratio — the lower, the more premium | Noir at ≤23% is among the most polished in the world |
| Session sake | Everyday, approachable drinking sake | Label 12 at 12% ABV and $30 |
| Food-friendly | Sake that enhances rather than competes with food | Core brand positioning — "sake doesn't fight with food" |
| The breakfast test | If sake smells fresh the next morning, it's high quality | All HEAVENSAKE is designed for this |
| Ochoko vs wine glass debate | Traditional cup vs modern stemware | HEAVENSAKE's non-negotiable: wine glass only |
| Funky yamahai | Bold, earthy, gamey sake style | Not HEAVENSAKE's style — we are clean, floral, elegant |
| Gift-worthy sake | Sake with premium packaging suitable for gifting | Label Noir and Prestige I in particular |
| Daily drinker | Reliable, affordable sake for regular consumption | Label 12 and Label Azur |
