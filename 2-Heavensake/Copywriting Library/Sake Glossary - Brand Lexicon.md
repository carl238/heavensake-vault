# Sake Glossary — HEAVENSAKE Brand Lexicon

## I. Brand Philosophy & Strategy

**Assemblage**
The defining artisanal technique of HEAVENSAKE. Borrowed from high-end French Champagne-making and introduced to sake by cellar master Régis Camus, it is the artistic layering of different sakes to create a complex, harmonious "third color" (like mixing blue and yellow to create purple).

**Blending**
The traditional, industrial counterpart to Assemblage. In HEAVENSAKE's vocabulary, "blending" is rejected as a term, as it implies mixing batches purely for taste uniformity, volume management, or cost-saving.

**Reviving (Not Redefining)**
The core mission of the brand. HEAVENSAKE expressly forbids the use of the marketing cliché "redefining sake." Instead, the goal is to *revive* a dormant UNESCO cultural heritage — the "drink of the gods" — and make it culturally relevant for the modern luxury world.

**Pure Elevation**
The elegant, public-facing value proposition of the brand. It represents the physical purity of the liquid (sulfite and additive-free) and the spiritual and social lift (elevation of status and coolness) experienced when drinking it.

**A Better High**
The visceral, lifestyle-driven "internal engine" of the brand. Used primarily for nightlife and celebration contexts, it refers to the physiological benefit of drinking pure Junmai sake: a cleaner, more euphoric buzz without the heaviness of wine or the sugar crash of spirits.

**Meeting of Masters**
The foundational concept of Franco-Japanese cultural exchange. It represents the collaboration between French *savoir-faire* (Régis Camus) and the profound, centuries-old traditions of Japanese brewing (the Tojis).

**The 15-Year Drop**
A storytelling concept highlighting that the pristine water used to brew the sake takes 15 years to filter through the Japanese mountains before it is used. It is referred to as "eternity in a drop."

**Assemblage of Moments**
The thematic concept for HEAVENSAKE's 10-year anniversary, celebrating how the brand has brought sake into versatile, modern luxury settings — from fashion shows and art galleries to Michelin-starred dining and nightclub takeovers.

## II. The Ritual & Terminology

**The Wine Glass Ritual**
HEAVENSAKE's non-negotiable rule for consumption. The sake must be poured into a large wine glass to allow the complex floral and fruity aromas unlocked by assemblage to breathe and evolve.

**Ochoko**
The traditional, small cylindrical ceramic sake cup. HEAVENSAKE explicitly rejects the *ochoko* because its shape removes 30-40% of the aromatic experience and functionally limits the beverage to a "sushi counter" rather than an elevated dining context.

**Junmai** (純米 / *joon-my*)
Pure rice sake with no additives, no added sugar, gluten-free, and sulfite-free. HEAVENSAKE exclusively produces Junmai sake. *"Nothing added. Nothing taken away."*

**Toji** (杜氏 / *toh-jee*)
A traditional Japanese sake brewmaster. The masters who collaborate with Régis Camus to produce the base liquids. *"Ancient knowledge, living and breathing."*

## III. Symbols & Aesthetics

**The Torii Gate**
A core visual symbol used in the brand's merchandising and 10-year anniversary assets. It represents the passage from the mundane, everyday reality into the elevated "Heaven" of the brand experience.

**The Daruma**
A traditional Japanese doll symbolizing perseverance. Used by HEAVENSAKE for its 10-year anniversary ritual, where the act of painting the doll's eyes represents celebrating past achievements and looking toward a new chapter.

**The Hexagon**
A graphic element integrated into the HEAVENSAKE logo, representing the structured, French heritage (*L'Hexagone*) meeting Japanese artistry.

**Pink Resonance**
The creative concept driving the *Hanami / Sakura Prestige* launch, symbolizing the mixture of red warmth and white clarity to evoke spring, rebirth, and fleeting elegance.

## VI. Sake Technical Terms (For Educational Copy)

**Daiginjo** (大吟醸 / *dai-gin-jo*) — *"Half the grain. Twice the grace."*
The pinnacle of the brewer's art. Sake made from rice polished to at least 50% of its original size. The result is a liquid of exceptional delicacy: luminous, floral, impossibly refined.

**Ginjo** (吟醸 / *gin-jo*) — *"The door left slightly open."*
Rice polished to at least 60%, yielding expressive aromas and a bright, fruit-forward character.

**Seimaibuai** (精米歩合 / *say-my-boo-eye*) — *"Subtract to elevate."*
The milling rate — the percentage of the rice grain remaining after polishing.

**Koji** (麹 / *koh-jee*) — *"The first whisper of what's to come."*
The invisible architect of sake. A mold cultivated on steamed rice whose enzymes break starches into fermentable sugars. Without koji, there is no sake.

**Namazake** (生酒 / *nah-mah-zah-keh*) — *"Alive in the glass."*
Unpasteurized sake — raw and vital, alive with the energy of the kura.

**Kura** (蔵 / *koo-rah*) — *"A cathedral of fermentation."*
The sake brewery. Literally translated as 'storehouse.'

**Kanpai** (乾杯 / *kahn-pie*) — *"The ritual begins here."*
The Japanese toast — literally 'dry cup.'

## VII. Philosophical Terms (For Editorial / PR Use)

**Shokunin** (職人 / *shoh-koo-nin*) — *"A life's work in a single sip."*
The Japanese concept of the dedicated artisan — a craftsperson who pursues mastery as a lifelong commitment.

**Wabi-Sabi** (侘寂 / *wah-bee sah-bee*) — *"Perfect in its imperfection."*
The Japanese aesthetic of finding beauty in imperfection and impermanence.

**Ma** (間 / *mah*) — *"The silence that completes the music."*
The Japanese concept of negative space — the pause, the breath, the silence between notes. In sake, *ma* is the finish.

**Monozukuri** (ものづくり / *moh-noh-zoo-koo-ree*) — *"Made with intention. Received with gratitude."*
The art and spirit of making things — the Japanese philosophy that elevates manufacturing into devotion.

**Ikigai** (生き甲斐 / *ee-kee-gai*) — *"The reason everything began."*
The Japanese concept of purpose — the reason to rise in the morning.

**En Bouche** — *"The finish that stays with you."*
A French wine term meaning 'in the mouth' — used at HEAVENSAKE to describe the full palate experience: texture, flavor, evolution, and most critically, length.

**Umami** (旨味 / *oo-mah-mee*) — *"The taste that makes the meal complete."*
The fifth taste — savory, deep, mouth-coating, singular. Sake's secret weapon at the table.
