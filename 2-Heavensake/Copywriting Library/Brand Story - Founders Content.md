# HeavenSake Brand Story

## BRAND STORY

The HEAVENSAKE brand story is rooted in a unique cultural exchange, born from the desire to merge the profound traditions of Japanese sake brewing with the elegance and *joie de vivre* of French Champagne-making.

The story unfolds across several key pillars:

---

### The Genesis: A French Vision for a Japanese Heritage

The concept was conceived about twelve years ago by three French friends and visionaries:
- **Carl Hirschmann** (an entrepreneur)
- **Etienne Russo** (a world-renowned fashion producer)
- **Benjamin Eymere** (CEO of L'Officiel)

They shared a deep passion for sake and believed it had enormous untapped global potential. However, they knew that to truly elevate sake, they needed a compelling narrative — a way to bring a "touch of French joie de vivre" to the category, similar to how Grey Goose transformed vodka.

---

### The Innovation: Bringing "Assemblage" to Sake

To execute this vision, the founders partnered with **Régis Camus**, the legendary chef-de-cave of Champagne house Piper-Heidsieck and an eight-time IWC "Sparkling Winemaker of the Year." Camus became the architect of HEAVENSAKE's defining innovation: pioneering the art of *Assemblage* in the sake world.

While traditional sake blending is typically done by breweries for practical reasons — like cost efficiency and taste uniformity (mixing blue and yellow to get green) — *Assemblage* is an artistic creation. Camus treats sake like musical components, layering them to unlock entirely new, harmonious flavor profiles and vibrant aromas that did not previously exist (creating a "third color" like purple).

---

### The Meeting of Masters

The brand's DNA is built on collaborations with some of Japan's most revered traditional breweries, including Dassai, Urakasumi, Shichiken, Niizawa, and the Naohiko Noguchi Sake Institute. Initially, introducing a French Champagne master to traditional Japanese Tojis (brewmasters) was met with skepticism, as blending high-end sake was practically unheard of. However, once the Japanese masters tasted the elevated, complex results of Camus's assemblage, skepticism turned to profound respect, with one Toji even referring to Camus as a "God of Sake."

---

### The Mission: Reviving an Icon

At its core, HEAVENSAKE is on a mission to "revive" sake, not simply redefine it. The founders view sake as the "drink of the gods" — a 20-century-old UNESCO cultural heritage that has unfortunately lost its appeal among younger generations in Japan. By positioning HEAVENSAKE as a global cultural luxury brand, they aim to save the industry by placing sake in unexpected, high-end environments: from Michelin-starred restaurants to exclusive nightclubs in Ibiza and Monaco, and backstage with iconic musicians.

---

### Purity and "A Better High"

HEAVENSAKE exclusively produces **Junmai sake**, meaning it is exceptionally pure — free of additives, added sugar, gluten, and sulfites, while being three times less acidic than wine. This commitment to purity fuels their promise of "A Better High" or "Pure Elevation," offering a mindful, health-conscious indulgence that leaves consumers feeling elevated without the physical heaviness of wine or the aggression of hard spirits.

---

### The Ritual

To fully honor this artistry, the brand completely rejects the traditional, small cylindrical *ochoko* cups. HEAVENSAKE mandates that its sake be served chilled in a **large wine glass**. This allows the liquid to breathe, fully expressing the complex floral and fruity aromas crafted through the assemblage process — proving that sake belongs on the white tablecloth right next to the world's finest wines.

---

## FOUNDERS CONTENT STRATEGY (LinkedIn)

From the Brand Bible:

> *"People buy from people. Use the founders (Carl or Etienne) to tell the struggle and vision of the brand. Discuss 'winning the Champions League' of business."*

**Topics to cover:**
- The struggle of saving a declining industry
- The reality of building a global luxury brand
- The "double niche" challenge (luxury + sake)
- The strategy behind winning the "Champions League" of business
- The 10-year anniversary reflection
- Honest vulnerability about what the brand got wrong and what changed

**Voice notes (Carl's voice):**
- Warm and direct
- Personally vulnerable when appropriate
- Never selling — always storytelling
- Opens doors, doesn't push through them

**Voice notes (Régis's voice):**
- Reflective and precise
- Thinks in sensory metaphors
- Doesn't sell — he reveals
- Contemplative, never promotional

**Post cadence:** 2-3 times per week, mixing deep-dive narratives with lifestyle content.
