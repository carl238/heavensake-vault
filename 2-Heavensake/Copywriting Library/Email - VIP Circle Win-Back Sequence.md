## Email 1 — Message from the Founder

**Sender:** Carl (Founder)
**Format:** Plain Text (Personal) — ZERO design, zero images. Must feel like a personal Gmail message.
**Subject Line:** "I owe you a conversation, (Name)"
**Preheader:** This isn't a newsletter. It's a question I should have asked sooner.
**Send Week:** Week 1
**Psych Levers:** Heritage + Exclusivity
**CTA:** Reply directly to this email — I read every one. Carl.
**Segment:** VIP Circle — clients inactive 6+ months

**COPY:**

---
From: Carl — Founder, HEAVENSAKE
Subject: I owe you a conversation, (Name)
Preheader: This isn't a newsletter. It's a question I should have asked sooner.
---

Dear (Name),

This is Carl.

I don't usually write these emails — most of what leaves our house goes through the team, and that's by design. But this one felt like it needed to come from me, because what I want to say isn't something a newsletter can carry.

HEAVENSAKE turns ten this year. A decade. When I think about that number, I don't think about the bottles or the awards or the restaurants. I think about the people who showed up first. The ones who tasted something unfamiliar, trusted it, and brought it to their own table. You are one of those people.

And I realize, looking back honestly, that we may not have honored that the way we should have.

We were building — fast, in every direction — and somewhere along the way, the people who mattered most became the ones we spoke to least. That's on us. And I'd like to change it.

So I'm writing to ask you something directly:

**What did we get right — and where did we fall short?**

I mean it. Was it the experience of ordering? The way we communicated? Something about the product itself? Or did life simply move in a different direction?

There's no survey, no form, no algorithm. Just this email and my genuine curiosity. If you have five minutes and the honesty to spare, I'd be grateful to hear from you.

Nothing is for sale today. This is just a door I'm reopening — and I hope you'll walk through it.

With real gratitude,

**Carl**
Founder, HEAVENSAKE

*P.S. — I read every reply personally. If you'd prefer a call, just say the word.*

---

**Internal Production Notes:**
- Format: Pure plain text. No images, no headers, no buttons, no unsubscribe footer styling.
- Personalization: (Name) via CRM merge tag.
- Reply-to: Carl's actual email or monitored alias.
- KPIs: Reply rate (target 5-8%), qualitative sentiment analysis of responses.
- Follow-up: Manually reply to every response within 48h. Tag respondents in HubSpot for Email 2 personalization.

## Email 3 — You're Invited (Monaco)

**Sender:** Regis Camus (Chef de Cave)
**Format:** Plain Text (Personal)
**Subject Line:** "An evening ten years in the making — your name is on the list"
**Send Week:** Week 7
**Psych Levers:** Exclusivity + Desire + Heritage
**CTA:** \[ Confirm Your Presence \] → Link to Typeform

**Angle:** EXCLUSIVE MONACO INVITATION — Regis personally invites the VIP segment to the 10th Anniversary celebration. The invitation is framed as a natural privilege — not an offer. The reader should feel their place was reserved before the email was even written. Connects to The Invisible Gate strategy: these VIPs bypass the public Whisper and Application phases entirely.

**Content Outline:**
1. Opener: 'I have news I've been waiting to share with you.'
2. The event: Monaco, June 2026. An intimate evening celebrating the decade. 150 guests.
3. The tone: 'This isn't a party. It's a reunion — the people who wrote this story with us.'
4. The invitation: 'Your name was on the list before the list existed. I'd love for you to be there.'
5. Minimal logistics: link to a concierge-style Typeform to confirm attendance and share preferences for the evening. Framed as courtesy, not qualification.

**The key emotion:** You belong here. You always have.

**SAMPLE PASSAGES:**

*Opening:*
"I have news I've been waiting to share with you. And I wanted you to hear it before the world does."

*The event:*
"This June, HEAVENSAKE celebrates ten years. Not with a launch. Not with a campaign. With a gathering — in Monaco — of the people who made this decade possible. One hundred and fifty guests. One evening. The kind of room where every person has a reason to be there."

*The invitation:*
"I'm not asking if you'd like to attend. I'm telling you that your place has been held. You were part of this story before anyone called it a story — and an evening like this only means something if the people inside the room are the ones who wrote it."

*Logistics:*
"The link below will take you to a short form — just to confirm your attendance and share any preferences for the evening (dietary, travel, company). Think of it as our concierge reaching out, not a registration."

*Close:*
"Some milestones are private. This is one of them. I hope to see you in Monaco."

## Email 4 — A Note from the Cellar

**Sender:** Regis Camus (Chef de Cave)
**Format:** Plain Text (Personal)
**Subject Line:** "I've been composing something I've never attempted before"
**Send Week:** Week 5
**Psych Levers:** Heritage + Exclusivity + Scarcity + Desire
**CTA:** No link. Just: 'We'll speak again soon. For now, I wanted you to know.'

**Angle:** PRESTIGE III — EARLY REVEAL — Régis writes directly to VIP clients for the first time ever. He describes the creative process behind Prestige III without fully revealing it. The tone is artisanal, intimate — a master sharing his work-in-progress with the few people who can truly appreciate it. Builds desire and scarcity through restraint, not hype.

**Internal Notes:** First time Régis writes directly to clients — this must feel rare and authentic. His voice is more poetic and technical than Carl's — less conversational, more contemplative. DO NOT mention price, launch date, or product name. The mystery is the message. This email is pure anticipation-building.

**SAMPLE PASSAGES:**

*Opening:*
"Carl asked me to write to you. I don't do this often. In my world, the liquid is the message — and I've always trusted it to speak on my behalf. But what I'm working on right now feels different enough that I wanted to tell you myself."

*On the process:*
"For months, I've been in conversation with a new partner — a brewery I've admired for years. The work is delicate. Every assemblage begins as a question: what harmony exists here that hasn't been heard yet? With Prestige I, the answer came from Noguchi-san's extraordinary personal collection. With Prestige II, from Niizawa's resilience and precision. This time, the answer is coming from somewhere I haven't been before."

*On exclusivity:*
"I'm told the first expression will be reserved for the people Carl calls 'the ones who were here before the story was written.' I like that. It feels right. The people who understood what we were doing before anyone else should be the first to taste where it's going."

*Close:*
"I'll have more to share soon. For now, know that something is being composed — and it's being composed with you in mind."

## Email 6 — Welcome Back Home

**Sender:** HEAVENSAKE Team
**Format:** Minimal Design
**Subject Line:** "The house is different now — and it's because of you"
**Send Week:** Week 11
**Psych Levers:** Heritage + Exclusivity + Ritual
**CTA:** \[ Discover Club Heaven \] → Membership landing page

**The emotion:** Homecoming. Full circle. The house has changed — and they're the reason.

**Content Outline:**
1. Opener: 'Over the past weeks, we've received hundreds of replies. Every single one has changed us.'
2. What we learned: 2-3 concrete insights from feedback
3. What has changed: new communication approach, more intimate experiences, structured priority access.
4. Club Heaven: 'For people like you, we've created something that doesn't have a price — because it isn't for sale. It's a permanent invitation.'
5. Close: 'Welcome back home.'

**SAMPLE PASSAGES:**

*Opening:*
"Over the past two months, something unexpected happened. Carl opened a door — and hundreds of you walked through it. The feedback you shared was generous, sometimes tough, and always honest. We want you to see what it changed."

*What we heard (use real quotes when available):*
"One of you wrote: *'The sake is extraordinary. The experience of buying it felt like an afterthought.'* Another said: *'I stopped hearing from you — and I assumed you stopped caring.'* A third: *'I'd come back in a heartbeat if you made me feel like I mattered.'*

You were right. All of you."

*What changed:*
"Here's what's different now: We've rebuilt how we communicate — fewer broadcasts, more conversations. We've created a concierge channel for our closest clients. We've restructured our allocation system so the people who have been here longest are always served first. And we've designed a new kind of relationship for the people who helped us get here."

*Club Heaven:*
"We're calling it Club Heaven. It's not a loyalty program. There are no tiers, no points, no algorithms. It's access — to limited releases before they're public, to private experiences designed for forty people instead of four hundred, and to the people behind our bottles who rarely step into the spotlight. It doesn't have a price because it isn't for sale. It's our way of saying: you belong here."

*Close:*
"Welcome back home.
- The HEAVENSAKE Team

*P.S. — Carl wanted us to add this: 'Thank you for being honest with me. It's the most valuable thing anyone has given this house in ten years.'*"
