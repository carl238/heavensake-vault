## ENDO KAZUTOSHI & HEAVENSAKE
### WHERE PRECISION MEETS PLAYFULNESS

Endo Kazutoshi is a third-generation sushi master from Yokohama, trained in the centuries-old Edomae technique. He spent years as Executive Chef at Zuma, overseeing restaurants from London to Dubai, before opening Endo at The Rotunda in April 2019. Six months later, he earned a Michelin star.

But what sets Endo-san apart isn't just his skill, it's his spirit.

"I don't really see myself as a chef, I see myself as a craftsman," he says. "Every day is about making sushi better than the day before; it's never-ending." Yet beneath that relentless pursuit of perfection is something else: joy. Warmth. A refusal to take himself too seriously, even while taking his craft deadly seriously. This is why HEAVENSAKE and Endo-san are natural partners.

Endo-san's philosophy follows ichi-go ichi-e, "one time, one meeting," creating unique experiences that cannot be repeated. But unlike the somber temples of traditional omakase, there's lightness here. Conversation. Laughter between courses. He's a showman, explaining each finely crafted element in an engaging, charming way. This balance, between reverence and playfulness, discipline and spontaneity, is exactly what HEAVENSAKE embodies. We honor Japanese tradition while infusing it with French joie de vivre.

### ENDO IN THE SUN

At our Monaco gathering, Endo-san prepared an afternoon of nigiri paired with HEAVENSAKE, using Yu Kurosaki knives. Sake flowed. Japanese music floated in the background while Akiko-san glided through the space in her kimono. Precision and pleasure in perfect balance, the way sake was always meant to be enjoyed.

### THE MASTERS WHO CHOOSE HEAVENSAKE

From Kurosaki-san to Sushi Nakazawa, the world's finest omakase counters pour HEAVENSAKE, not because we asked, but because they recognize what we do for the sake industry. Like them, we obsess over every detail: the breweries, the rice polishing, the Assemblage, the finish. But unlike traditional sake, we bring something more: a sense of celebration. A lighter touch, an elevation, "A BETTER HIGH."

### FROM THE COUNTER TO THE CLOUDS

HEAVENSAKE and Endo-san share the same mission: to elevate tradition without losing its soul. To honor craft while embracing life. Because the greatest omakase isn't just about perfection, it's about connection. And that's worth raising a glass to.

## ENRIQUE OLVERA & HEAVENSAKE
### SAKE AND MEXICAN GASTRONOMY: A NEW ERA

> "We sought a sake with the architectural integrity to complement the earthy soul of our cuisine. Label Orange is a revelation of elegance—creamy yet crystalline." — Enrique Olvera

In the realm of high craftsmanship, certain encounters are dictated by a shared vibration. The collaboration between HEAVENSAKE and Chef Enrique Olvera is the result of such an intuition — a meeting of minds where Japanese culture and the soulful depth of modern Mexican gastronomy find a common language.

### The Visionary Behind Pujol

Enrique Olvera is a definitive architect of modern flavor. As the visionary behind Pujol, a two-Michelin-starred restaurant consistently celebrated on the global stage, Olvera has redefined the narrative of Mexican cuisine. His work is a masterclass in emotional clarity: rooted in ancestral heritage, yet executed with a contemporary sharpness that resonates profoundly with the HEAVENSAKE philosophy.

### An Unconventional Sake Pairing in NYC

The collaboration unfolded in New York City during an exclusive residence celebrating Pujol's 25th anniversary. Within this milestone, a new dialogue was born. Rather than treating sake as a guest to the table, Chef Olvera embraced it as a structural equal, a medium of nuance, restraint, and profound textural resonance.

### Pairing HEAVENSAKE Label Orange with Pipián

The centerpiece of this culinary journey was the HEAVENSAKE Label Orange, orchestrated to accompany a cauliflower composition elevated by pipián, a traditional Mexican sauce of deep complexity.

For Olvera, the choice was an exercise in equilibrium:
- **The Challenge:** The dish demanded a partner with presence.
- **The Solution:** A sake that could support the weight of the earth while maintaining alpine finesse.
- **The Result:** Label Orange's signature assemblage provided the perfect counterpoint, revealing the true essence of the grain.

### A Shared Philosophy: The Art of Restraint

At its core, this union is a celebration of the "Essential." HEAVENSAKE's dedication to harmony mirrors Olvera's culinary vernacular, where raw materials are treated with reverence. While pairing sake with Mexican flavors may appear avant-garde, the result is a seamless fluidity that transcends geography.

This collaboration is a manifesto for the evolving global table. To see one of the world's most influential chefs integrate sake into the lexicon of Mexican fine dining is a radical statement of openness. It is a reminder that when true craft leads the way, borders dissolve into a singular experience.

## MEET THE MASTER: MAMIMOZI AND CALLIGRAF2ITY

Based in the vibrant heart of Shibuya, Tokyo, MAMIMOZI is a contemporary Japanese calligrapher who transforms classical script into singular artifacts of connection. Her journey began at the age of nine, grounding her practice in the rigorous discipline of classical Kanji. Yet her creative spirit felt a pull toward the raw, kinetic energy of global street culture.

From this creative friction, her signature style, "Calligraf2ity" was born. A fusion of Japanese calligraphy and graffiti, where the meditative structure and precision of the brush meet the rhythmic scale and vibrant energy of street art. It is not a compromise between two worlds. It is a new language entirely.

MAMIMOZI has since become a definitive voice of "Japanese Cool," with collaborations spanning 40 countries and partnerships with some of the world's most visionary brands. Her work lives at the intersection of tradition and disruption, precisely the space where HEAVENSAKE was born.

### A Visual Prayer: The Philosophy of the Connection

We had the honor to witness the creation of a 1-of-1 original calligraphy scroll that transcends traditional collaboration. This work is not merely art, it is a rare artifact of MAMIMOZI's soul, capturing visceral moments of human connection through brushwork that carries the full weight of her discipline and the full freedom of her imagination.

「Heart-to-Heart」(心と心) inspired by the shared heartbeat of mother and child, this piece captures the precise moment where two independent hearts overlap in mirrored, perfect balance. A visual prayer for a love that is both grounded and infinite, speaking to the universal gravity that pulls two souls together.

Sharing this singular expression of human creativity is our way of honoring the dedication, love, and passion that Japanese artists pour into their craft. It is our tradition to revere contemporary masters of artistry, and we invite you to join us in this celebration of beauty and connection, as we continue our quest for the purest expressions of culture.

## RM SOTHEBY'S & HEAVENSAKE
### A SURREAL ASSEMBLAGE OF SUPERCARS AND SAKE IN ABU DHABI

In the world of luxury, true appreciation is found in the details — the curve of a fender, the clarity of a liquid, the mastery of a craft. This month, HEAVENSAKE had the privilege of joining RM Sotheby's for their prestigious Collectors Week in Abu Dhabi, marking a defining moment in the global sake revolution.

Surrounded by the surreal beauty of the St. Regis Saadiyat and a curation of the world's most coveted automobiles, we hosted the first-ever RM Sotheby's & HEAVENSAKE Sake Omakase. It was an "assemblage" in the truest sense: a harmony of Japanese tradition, automotive excellence, and the modern luxury lifestyle.

### The Setting: An Intersection of Icons

The atmosphere was electric, defined by a distinct "Assemblage" of cultures. We designed a bespoke sake bar to reflect the stunning architecture of the St. Regis and the extraordinary machinery on display.

Guests sipped our Junmai Daiginjo amidst automotive legends, most notably the Ferrari F40. The juxtaposition was intentional. Much like the F40's radical approach to engineering, Junmai Sake is a marvel of Japanese perfection and discipline.

### The Master: Taca-san and the Art of Omotenashi

An exceptional liquid requires an exceptional guide. To ensure the authenticity of the experience, we were honored to welcome Taca-san, owner of Tokyo's iconic sake bar, TWELV.

Flying directly from Japan, Taca-san brought the spirit of Omotenashi (Japanese hospitality) to the UAE. His presence underscored our commitment to the "Meeting of Masters," bridging the gap between the hidden alleyways of Tokyo and the grandiose shoreline of Abu Dhabi. Under his guidance, guests discovered that sake, when served in the proper glassware, rivals the complexity of the world's finest wines.

### The Guests: F1 Royalty and High-Octane Company

The synergy between high-performance cars and high-performance liquids drew notable attention. We were delighted to welcome Formula 1 driver Esteban Ocon, who stopped by to greet the team alongside our friends at Pirelli.

## TWELV. & HEAVENSAKE
### FRIENDSHIP, TOKYO, SAKE

Nine years ago, two brands met in Tokyo with the same impossible dream: making sake the next big thing in the alcohol world.

Back then, sake was still shrouded in mystery, intimidating to most, misunderstood by many, relegated to the back corner even in Japanese restaurants.

Today, sake is the new cool kid on the block. And a lot of that shift happened because of brands like TWELV and HEAVENSAKE refusing to accept the old narrative.

### Taca-san & the Birth of TWELV.

Norihiro Tacamizuma, better known as Taca-san, founded TWELV., one of Tokyo's most iconic sake bars, with a singular mission: to strip away the pretension and bring sake into the modern world. Not by dumbing it down, but by celebrating its depth and versatility in the heart of a hidden speakeasy.

TWELV. isn't a stuffy tasting room. It's a living, breathing space where sake flows as freely as conversation, where curious beginners sit alongside seasoned connoisseurs.

Taca-san represents the new generation of sake lovers: knowledgeable but unpretentious, deeply rooted in tradition but unafraid to experiment, serious about quality but allergic to snobbery.

He's exactly the kind of person HEAVENSAKE was made for.

### Nine Years. One Vision.

When the founders of HEAVENSAKE and TWELV. first crossed paths, the connection was immediate. Both were outsiders in their own way — HEAVENSAKE bringing French Assemblage to Japanese sake, TWELV. bringing Tokyo bar culture to a drink that had grown distant from young Japanese audiences.

Over the years, that shared vision turned into friendship. Late-night tastings. Collaborative events. Endless conversations about the future of the category.

### The Varsity Jacket: Effortless Tokyo Cool

The HEAVENSAKE x TWELV. varsity jacket is a symbol. Designed to capture the effortless cool of Tokyo's bar scene, it unites a classic varsity silhouette with HEAVENSAKE's unique sense of design — versatile. Timeless. A little rebellious. Just like sake should be.

### Redefining a 2,000-Year Legacy

Taca-san and HEAVENSAKE are part of a movement redefining what sake means in the 21st century. Not by abandoning its heritage, but by making it desirable.

From Tokyo to Paris, New York to Ibiza, sake is having its moment, from TWELV Tokyo to the world.

Nine years of friendship. One shared mission. This is HEAVENSAKE x TWELV.

## AMBUSH & HEAVENSAKE
### AN ODE TO JAPANESE MINIMALISM

HEAVENSAKE has partnered with Tokyo's iconic fashion brand AMBUSH® to celebrate the artistry of sake culture and contemporary design. This collaboration culminates in a unique collection of sake cups, crafted to honor the heritage of Japanese sake while embracing HEAVENSAKE & AMBUSH®'s bold, innovative aesthetics in a limited edition run of 100 sets worldwide.

Inspired by the revered artistry of antique ceramic sake cups, HEAVENSAKE Creative Director Carl Hirschmann infused this collaboration with the bold aesthetic that defines Yoon Ahn's experimental AMBUSH® jewelry line. Each cup's sleek silhouette reflects AMBUSH®'s futuristic energy alongside the timeless elegance of HEAVENSAKE.

The cups are crafted from tin, one of the finest materials for drinking sake, known for its ability to enhance aroma and maintain temperature. The sleek, futuristic silhouette reflects both brands' signature styles: bold yet refined, honoring heritage while redefining what a sake vessel should be.

The core idea behind these objects is for them to be perceived as **jewelry pieces and serve as conversation starters**, a true bridge between heritage and innovation.

## YU KUROSAKI & HEAVENSAKE
### WHERE STEEL MEETS SPIRIT

In Japan, the knife is not merely a tool. It is an extension of the hand, a meditation in steel, a bridge between intention and creation.

Yu Kurosaki is the youngest blacksmith ever to be awarded the title of Master by the Japanese government, an honor that recognizes not just skill, but the preservation and evolution of centuries-old tradition. Born in 1979 in Takefu Echizen, he began his journey at sixteen, abandoning formal education to pursue his calling under master blacksmith Hiroshi Kato, completing a rigorous twelve-year apprenticeship.

Today, operating from his minimalist workshop in the storied Takefu Knife Village, a 700-year-old hub of Japanese blade-making, Kurosaki-san creates knives that are as much art as instruments. His signature Fujin line, with its hand-hammered wind patterns, and Shizuku collection, featuring drop-shaped tsuchime finishes, have earned him a cult following among the world's greatest chefs and collectors.

This limited edition set was Kurosaki's way to celebrate the 9-year anniversary of Heavensake. Rumor has it that something special is being prepared for the 10-year celebration...
