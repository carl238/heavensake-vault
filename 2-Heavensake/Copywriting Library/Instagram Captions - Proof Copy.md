## 5. LABEL AZUR by Shichiken

The pristine waters of Hakushu flow through the new LABEL AZUR, an Assemblage by Regis Camus in collaboration with the legendary Shichiken brewery.

Brought to life by centuries of savoir-faire from the Kitahara family, this Junmai Ginjo is our ode to purity, elegance and poetic rebellion.

The name "Shichiken," meaning "Seven Sages," was bestowed in 1835, inspired by a pair of fanlights titled "Seven Sages of the Bamboo Grove" gifted by the Lord of Takatō Castle. This name reflects the brewery's deep cultural roots and reverence for tradition.

Beyond brewing, Shichiken is committed to sustainability and community engagement. Their practices include circular sake-making, where by-products like sake lees are repurposed, and initiatives to reduce CO2 emissions through renewable energy sources. The brewery also hosts annual events like the Daigahara-shuku Market and Kura Festival, fostering local connections and celebrating regional culture.

Visitors to Shichiken can experience its rich history firsthand. The brewery's main building, which once accommodated Emperor Meiji during his 1880 tour, now serves as a museum. Guests can also dine at Restaurant Daimin, savoring dishes prepared with fermentation techniques, and enjoy koji-based desserts at the Kura café.

In essence, Shichiken stands as a testament to the harmonious blend of tradition and modernity, offering a sake experience that is both authentic and forward-thinking.

## 10. ARITA BOTTLE — Prestige I

Designed by HEAVENSAKE and produced by ARITA PORCELAIN LAB, "PRESTIGE I" pays homage to the centuries-old bond between sake and ceramics. Conceived as a sculptural vessel, it honors Japanese craftsmanship by drawing on the glaze techniques and color palettes of some of the most iconic vintage sake cups. Its luminous pale blue hue, born from iron-infused glaze, evokes serene purity, while the raw white base honors the earth. The drop shaped mouth is an homage to sake's main ingredient: water. From laminated corks that preserve the sake's aroma to meticulous clay mixing, mud casting, and hand-drilling, PRESTIGE I is a testament to the history of Japanese porcelain. More than a vessel, it is a work of art, blending tradition and modernity for the joy of our senses.

## Caption Patterns & Notes

**Structural signature elements:**
- Capitalized poetic lines for product announcements ("THE ENTRY IS MAJESTIC, COMPLEX AND DELICATE ALIKE")
- Lists of guests/collaborators as a form of "name roll call" — cultural signal
- Brand mission woven naturally: "save a dying industry," "revive sake," "cultural exchange"
- Always leads with story/context, never product first
- Closes with inclusive invitation: "we invite you to stay close," "we raise our glasses"

**Tone notes:**
- More lyrical and poetic than LinkedIn
- Short paragraphs for scroller readability
- Rich sensory language: "floated around with her kimono," "quiet soul behind the ritual"
- Historical context anchors the modern moment: "Since 2016..."
