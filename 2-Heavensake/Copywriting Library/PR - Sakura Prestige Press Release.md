## PRESS RELEASE DRAFT

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

### HEAVENSAKE introduces Prestige Sakura
#### Pink Resonance, composed for the Hanami moment

**Paris / Tokyo — Spring 2026** — Spring doesn't arrive with noise. It arrives with a shift—light changes, air softens, and the world turns pink for a brief, unforgettable window.

Today, HEAVENSAKE unveils **Prestige Sakura**: a limited-edition expression created for Hanami, the season when Japan pauses to honor beauty precisely because it doesn't last.

---

### Pink Resonance: warmth + clarity, felt in the glass

Prestige Sakura is guided by **Pink Resonance**—the meeting of red warmth and white clarity into something that can't be explained as a color trend. It's a vibration. A reset. A quiet sense of rebirth.

Housed in a sanded matte pink ceramic vessel crafted with the artisans of **Arita Porcelain Lab**, Prestige Sakura is designed as an object that belongs on the table—touched, poured, remembered.

---

### The liquid: Japanese mastery, elevated through Assemblage

Prestige Sakura is created with Japan's acclaimed **Niizawa Brewery** and composed by **Régis Camus**, HEAVENSAKE's Master of Assemblage. Borrowed from the cellars of Champagne and brought into sake with respect, Assemblage is composition—layering liquids until a new harmony appears, a "third color" that didn't exist before.

As with every HEAVENSAKE expression, Prestige Sakura is **Junmai**: nothing added, nothing taken away. The result is floral lift, a silky glide on the palate, and a finish that lingers like the last petal letting go.

---

### The ritual: wine glass, chilled, unhurried

Prestige Sakura is made to open up. Serve it chilled, in a large wine glass—never a small traditional cup—so the aromas can rise and evolve the way they were composed to.

---

### A toast to the hands behind the season

Prestige Sakura also honors the women whose craft and vision shape this world—those who create, refine, and carry tradition forward without turning it into performance.

> "Hanami is a lesson in presence," says Régis Camus. "You can't hold it—you can only experience it. Prestige Sakura is my way of composing that feeling: luminous, delicate, alive."
