# 10th Anniversary Strategy

The HEAVENSAKE 10th anniversary is not a celebration — it is a reset. Ten years of remarkable brand moments assembled into a global narrative moment that proves sake belongs everywhere. The conceptual frame is "Assemblage of Everything."

---

## The Conceptual Framework: Assemblage of Everything

Carl's most sophisticated brand communications insight: the assemblage concept that defines HEAVENSAKE's sake (blending different rice varieties, breweries, and vintages into something greater than any part) applies to the brand's entire 10-year biography.

> "Assemblage is not only for sake but is also for other aspects of HEAVENSAKE in the last 10 years. It might be assemblage of liquid, assemblage of place — because we celebrate all over the world: Fred, Monaco, Japan, Europe, Miami. Assemblage of people — it's related with designer, chef, Toji. Assemblage of culture — HEAVENSAKE is contaminated with music, with arts, with design. And the last one is the assemblage of desire — we create something that the customer deserves. They want prestige. We create scarcity."
> — *2026-02-04 Hanami Launch & 10th Anniversary Strategy*

**The five assemblages:**
1. **Assemblage of Liquid** — the sake itself, blended across breweries and rice varieties
2. **Assemblage of Place** — Monaco, Japan, Milan, Paris, Miami, Dubai, New York
3. **Assemblage of People** — Régis, the Tojis, designers, chefs, cultural figures
4. **Assemblage of Culture** — music, art, design, nightlife, gastronomy
5. **Assemblage of Desire** — scarcity-driven luxury that the right customer feels they deserve

This framework drives all communications, event design, merchandise, and product strategy for the anniversary.

## The Daruma Ritual: Brand Storytelling

The Daruma ritual is HEAVENSAKE's most powerful piece of cultural content for the anniversary. It operates as a visual story arc across two events.

**Daruma A — Closing the Chapter (May 2026, Chichiken, Japan)**
- Black/white Daruma covered in washi paper bearing archival images from 10 years of HEAVENSAKE
- Mommy-san (calligrapher) closes the first eye at the water source at Chichiken forest
- Location: the source — going to the water, honoring what created the sake
- Carl: "I want Mommy by the water. I want her through that Torii gate. I want the Daruma. And we're closing that chapter in the forest at Chichiken. Honoring the water."

**Daruma B — Opening the Future (November 2026, Tokyo)**
- Pink Daruma (Prestige 3 color); possibly different calligrapher to reach different demographics
- Eye-opening ceremony at October Japan event
- Video arc: Part 1 ends with Mommy's brush nearly touching the pink Daruma eye → teaser → Part 2 is the full opening ceremony in Japan

**Design specifications:**
- Face: Tsumasaki (front), "luck" (back) in kanji — calligraphy coordinated with Mommy-san
- Side panels: Shibari imagery (culturally resonant; references 2016 Paris launch)
- Motifs: Phoenix (Carl has a personal relationship with the phoenix) and Dragon
- No printing on the face — clean
