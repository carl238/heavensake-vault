## Carl's Philosophy & Thinking

Carl's core reframing of the entire e-commerce strategy:

> "Priority on existing network first: before scaling with ads, you want a full audit of who is already in your world (Clubheaven, Shopify customers, extended personal network) and how valuable they are in spend, influence, and future potential."

His rejection of mass communication in favor of radical personalization:

> "Generic newsletters are seen as obsolete compared to cheap, personalized emails written and sent at scale with AI and light human oversight."

On why the Monaco F1 party is the emotional anchor of everything:

> "Nobody will pay 5k for 'membership benefits' alone, but they will move for access to a legendary party plus a halo of global events and allocations."

His critique of the team's "2-move thinking" on the RSVP funnel:

> "You repeatedly challenged '2-move' thinking (e.g., simple ad → form → party logic) and pushed for full journey design, including fallout (people rejected for the party, how they feel, how they are re-engaged)."

On why VIPs won't fill a long application form:

> "The top 20% (people you are grateful to already have) will rarely complete applications; those who do are often not the 'best' ones."

On the risk of rejection creating brand enemies:

> "Making people 'apply for a party' and then rejecting them creates enemies of the brand, unlike lotteries for rare bottles where losing feels like bad luck, not personal rejection."

On the sequence problem — must fix the foundation before scaling:

> "Launching cold Meta ads for 5k memberships before you can even onboard basic customers properly is misaligned (welcome emails wrong, brand story off, no clear care plan)."

On existing customer skew (Label Noir dominance):

> "Early sales driven by your personal network, who knew Noir was the best and it launched first. A past bad batch of Azur hurt its reputation."

On RFM vs. reality in customer segmentation:

> "Pure spend/recency misses 'diamonds in the rough' (low spend but huge influence, or one bad experience but high potential). You need external data: LinkedIn, Instagram, news articles, roles, industries."

On the inner circle care philosophy:

> "Prestige buyers: send flowers on Valentine's Day, or other highly personal gestures, plus a special follow-up on their Prestige experience and early access to Prestige 3 (possibly gifting one bottle)."

> "Tailored communication acknowledging past issues (Prestige 1 had some problems) and framing Prestige 3 as a thank-you and redemption arc."

On brand narrative misalignment in onboarding:

> "Onboarding emails today are misaligned (e.g., starting with Assemblage instead of the mission to restore sake's place), so core copy and flows must be fixed to reflect the true story before scaling."

## Key Strategic Decisions

### Strategic Principles Agreed
1. **Existing network first** — full AI audit before any ad spend
2. **Ultra-personalization over mass** — personalized emails at scale beat generic newsletters
3. **Monaco F1 party as core magnet** — the emotional anchor of everything
4. **Segmentation by use case:** VIPs already known / "cool" social influencers / real high-spend prospects (sake buyers, finance, HNWI) / broad cold audience
5. **Think 5 moves ahead** — not just ad → form → party, but full journey including rejection paths and re-engagement

### RSVP / Party Funnel — Refined Direction
- **Two separate funnels:** High-friction application for cold audience; low-friction concierge for VIPs
- **Video hook:** Past Monaco party footage (Will.i.am, glamorous scenes) to attract cool people
- **Segment explicitly:** "Cool but low-spend" vs "real potential customers" (finance, F1, HNWI)
- Form/HubSpot infrastructure: keep it, but make it modular — different versions for cold vs warm audiences

### Membership & $5K Offer
- **Package:** Access to global events (Monaco, Tokyo, New York, Miami) + annual allocation + limited editions + party access
- **Framing:** People should feel part of a global sake movement, not "paying to access a party"
- **Sequence:** Only push $5K tier after inner circle mapping and care flows are live and tested
- **Target:** ClubHeaven members first; outreach only by invitation initially

### Customer Base & AI Audit
- ~600–689 Shopify records; Label Noir/Azur historically ~70% of mix
- AI audit already run on first ~60 top customers: classified into C-suite executives, media, finance, surgeons, entrepreneurs; flagged "at risk high-influence" and "diamonds in the rough" (high spend, low visibility)
- **Next step:** Full audit of entire database — not just top 60
- Combine RFM with external influence data (LinkedIn, Instagram, press)

### Product Mix Logic
- Product mix should be driven by: margin and role (brand builder vs profit driver) + target persona and channel
- Prestige 3 needs dedicated strategy to justify outsized revenue contribution
- Jūmi 12 is a unique hook (lower alcohol, less sugar) — not just "another SKU"

### Meta Ads
- Need ~50 optimization events/week for stable learning; current 1,000 €/month budget is below threshold
- Recommendation: increase Meta budget, cut programmatic, focus on add-to-cart first
- Priority: fix onboarding and care flows before scaling ads

### Inner Circle Care Program
- **Top 5 Prestige whales** — personal gestures: flowers, early access, gifted bottle of Prestige 3
- **Top 50 champions/whales** — exclusive previews, personal invites, check-in emails
- **Top 200** — promising + influential, tailored outreach
- **Operational need:** "Young, smart, tech-friendly" operator (~2K €/month in Italy) to run AI tools, data cleaning, list building, personalized outreach

## Brand DNA Insights

- **The party is the brand in motion:** Monaco isn't just an event — it's the emotional proof that HEAVENSAKE belongs at the highest level of global luxury life. Everything in the e-commerce strategy flows from this.
- **Brand narrative must change BEFORE scaling:** Onboarding with Assemblage technique is wrong. Should start with the mission: restoring sake's rightful place in the world. This is a brand voice correction that precedes any growth.
- **Diamonds in the rough:** Some of the most valuable people in the HEAVENSAKE network may have only bought once or spent less. They're high-influence, private, and won't show up in pure RFM ranking. Carl's instinct is that human judgment + AI enrichment finds these people.
- **Monaco rejection creates enemies:** This insight is strategically profound. How you handle people who don't get into the party defines brand perception as much as how you treat those who do.
