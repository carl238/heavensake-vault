# US Market Intelligence from Marketing Meetings

> Intelligence brief synthesized from 68 market intelligence entries across Monday Marketing Meeting transcripts (June 2025 – March 2026). This document is an analytical synthesis, not a raw list. Patterns, trends, and strategic implications are highlighted throughout.

---

## Executive Summary

The US premium sake market in 2025–2026 is navigating three simultaneous pressures: a broad alcohol industry decline, a stalling sake category, and mid-tier dining under structural stress. HEAVENSAKE's data shows the brand is performing **above category** ("sake is up two digits"), but fragile distribution dynamics, concentrated competitor pressure from IWA/LVMH, and a widening gap between omakase slowdown and thriving sake-bar culture require market-by-market tactical precision.

The clearest opportunity: wine and champagne are retreating among high-net-worth consumers exactly when premium sake has proven itself to ultra-luxury venues (Sotheby's Abu Dhabi, Aman Hotels, Ritz Carlton Japan). The brand must convert this cultural moment into systemic distribution gains before the window narrows.

## 1. Macro Environment

### Alcohol Industry Decline
- **Source:** December 2025 and July 2025 meetings
- Industry-wide alcohol consumption is declining. Southern Glazer's owner projected the industry "will likely suffer through end of 2025 and 2026," with wine suffering more than spirits.
- Health awareness growing: consumers increasingly aware of alcohol's health impacts (brain inflammation data). Mushrooms and psychedelics gaining scientific credibility as alternatives.
- [[Carl]]'s strategic thesis: *"People will drink less and drink smarter. In the quest for drinking smarter they will come across sake."* Consumer shift from "wanting to look good" to "wanting to feel good" (parallel observation from Golden Goose CEO). Sake positioned as healthier than hard spirits mixed with sugary sodas.
- Carl's market timeline: **5–10 more years** before sake crossover to mass market.

### Sake Category Dynamics
- Sake category is "slightly up" overall — one of few beverage alcohol categories growing. HEAVENSAKE reportedly **up year-over-year** versus category.
- Big alcohol groups (large spirits companies) pressuring distributors (Southern, Barbain) to move their own stock, squeezing out smaller sake brands. Budget cuts needed industry-wide.
- HEAVENSAKE's clear advantage: brand equity and assemblage story resonate far beyond raw category dynamics.

### Opportunity: Wine and Champagne Decline
- Carl observed: declining wine and champagne bookings among sommeliers and HNW consumers — "from one or two big bookings a week to one or two a month."
- Young consumers not interested in heavy wines.
- **Strategic implication:** Perfect timing to recruit wine-world ambassadors and sommeliers transitioning from declining champagne/wine focus. Sake positioned as "the next chapter for wine lovers."

## 2. Market-by-Market Breakdown

### New York

**Current status: Soft but recovering**

- [[Nes Rueda]] reported **8% sales decline** in NYC (as of March 2026). Harsh winter. Heavy competition.
- Some NYC accounts losing share to competitors — Sushi by Bu "has a bit of a thing going" with Brooklyn Kura and Sake Ono. Some accounts down 50% for HEAVENSAKE.

**Distribution challenge:**
- Many small sake bars loyal to Mutual Trading — won't switch distributors. This is a genuine structural barrier.

**Top accounts and strategy:**

**Key intel:**

## 5. Consumer Trends & Insights

### Generational divide
- **45+ audience:** appreciates HEAVENSAKE bottle design and branding; brand appreciation drives purchase
- **Younger consumers:** more focused on flavor; brand seen as too expensive by some Japanese omakase account buyers
- **Gen Z:** increasingly declining alcohol at events entirely; non-purchasing participant demographic
- **Millennials+:** still purchasing; key target

### Omakase trend softening
- Consumer spending on omakase slowing. Harder to sell seats than before.
- **Opportunity:** HEAVENSAKE can help omakase restaurants promote themselves — "helping fill seats on slow nights." The ICA Sake Dinner model proved this: both seatings sold out, restaurant grateful.

### The "date night" positioning
- [[Carl]]'s insight: sake unites men and women better than champagne or red wine. "Date Night" concept approved as a key marketing pillar.
- Test concept: sponsor 3 couples for two date nights — one with champagne/red wine, one with sake — and document.

- [[Mio]] proposed wellness angle: connect HEAVENSAKE with Alo Yoga (billion-dollar brand); post-pilates class sake tastings at private members clubs.

### International traveler behavior
- Accounts with high international traveler traffic (Soho Houses, luxury hotels) are key — international travelers already know HEAVENSAKE from South of France and Ibiza; brand recognition immediately present.

## 8. Strategic Implications & Patterns

**Pattern 1: The premium-or-bust dynamic**
The US market is bifurcating sharply. Mid-tier is under pressure; premium is selective but present. HEAVENSAKE must lean unambiguously into premium positioning — the mid-market squeeze is structural, not cyclical.

**Pattern 2: Event-led distribution is more durable than cold placement**
The ICA dinner model, Mila omakase activation, Pogos Dallas sell-out, and Sotheby's Abu Dhabi all demonstrate that event-tied placements convert to long-term accounts. Cold distribution without activation produces slow depletion and eventual delisting.

**Pattern 3: Restaurant groups over individual accounts**
Once placed in major restaurant groups (IDG, SBE/Katsuya, TAO, Major Food Group), longevity follows. Single-account placements require more maintenance per unit of volume.

**Pattern 4: The bottle-as-barrier problem**
Bottle shape (height/fit in fridge) is a genuine obstacle in small venues — not a perception issue. This requires either a packaging solution or intentional account selection that avoids venues where it's a problem (i.e., target open shelving/display situations over tight refrigeration).

**Pattern 5: Japan momentum creates US credibility**
Japanese account buyers in the US visit the website first. Japan success (Ritz Carlton partnership, Shunji placement, Anniversary event) directly supports US sales. Investment in Japan market pays dividends in all markets.

**Pattern 6: IWA's capital raise is the tide that lifts all boats**
If LVMH is doubling down on premium sake, it validates the category for investors, retailers, and restaurant buyers. HEAVENSAKE should accelerate its own capital raise timing in response.
