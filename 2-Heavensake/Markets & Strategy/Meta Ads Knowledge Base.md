## The Seven Strategic Rules

These are non-negotiable for every HEAVENSAKE campaign on Meta:

### 1. Always Use Original Audience — Never Advantage+
Advantage+ overrides age-gating. Meta's algorithm can serve ads to users below 21, violating U.S. alcohol advertising law. This caused the March 21 rejection. Advantage+ also ballooned our audience from ~1M to 200M+ unqualified users.

**Exception:** Meta's Leads objective forces Advantage+ audience in the current UI (as of March 2026). In that case, set age (21+) and locations as "hard limits" under Controls — Advantage+ cannot override these.

### 2. Always 21+ in the U.S.
Legal drinking age. Non-negotiable. For other markets: 18 (Japan, EU), 25 (some markets). Always verify before launching a campaign in a new geography.

### 3. Geo-Focus on 6–10 High-Value Metros
A $300 budget across 330M Americans = noise. The same budget focused on 1M–15M qualified people in key metros = measurable brand impressions and meaningful frequency. Our historical best-performing campaigns used 4-state targeting (CA, FL, NY, TX).

### 4. Layer the Targeting
Single-dimension targeting (just interests, or just income) = either too broad or too narrow. The model:
- **Layer 1:** Relevant interests (sake, champagne, foodie, art)
- **Layer 2:** Purchase behavior (Engaged Shoppers)
- **Layer 3:** Income bracket (optional, by product tier)

### 5. Sequential Campaign Architecture
Engagement → Conversion. Never both simultaneously from day one.
- Campaign 1 builds social proof and populates Video Viewers / IG Engagers audiences
- Campaign 2 retargets those warmed audiences 7+ days later
- The engagement campaign's job is to fill the retargeting pool

### 6. Manual Placements Only — Facebook + Instagram
Audience Network and Messenger have weaker age verification, lower-quality traffic, and dilute brand positioning for a luxury product. Always uncheck Audience Network, Messenger, Threads, WhatsApp.

### 7. The Régis Camus / Champagne Angle Is a Targeting Weapon
PIPER-HEIDSIECK, Dom Pérignon, Krug, and champagne interests reach exactly the type of consumer who would pay $92+ for an assemblage sake. This is HEAVENSAKE's unique targeting edge — no other sake brand has a champagne cellar master story.

## Alcohol Compliance Checklist

Use this before publishing ANY HEAVENSAKE campaign:

- [ ] Audience type: Original Audience (NOT Advantage+)?
- [ ] Minimum age: 21+ (U.S.)?
- [ ] Placements: Manual — Facebook + Instagram only?
- [ ] Audience Network: unchecked?
- [ ] Messenger: unchecked?
- [ ] Creative: No depiction of excessive drinking?
- [ ] Creative: No minors visible?
- [ ] Creative: No health claims?
- [ ] Special Ad Category: None?

### What Happened When We Got It Wrong (March 21, 2026)
- Used Advantage+ audience → Meta overrode 25+ age-gating
- Audience ballooned to 200M+ (should have been ~1M)
- Ad rejected within hours for alcohol policy violation
- Appeal submitted → takes 2 days → campaign dead for the window
- **Lesson:** Always verify Original Audience before clicking Publish

## Campaign Architecture

### Sequential Model (Standard)
```
Week 1-2:  Campaign 1 (Engagement)
           └── Builds social proof
           └── Populates Video Viewers 75% audience
           └── Populates IG Engagers audience

Week 2-4:  Campaign 2 (Traffic/Conversion)
           └── Retargets warmed audiences from Campaign 1
           └── Drives to product page
           └── Uses "Learn More" CTA + UTM-tagged URL
```

### Budget Framework
| Budget | Structure |
|---|---|
| $300–$600 | Single campaign, single ad set, one creative |
| $600–$1,500 | Two campaigns (engagement → conversion), 2 ad sets each |
| $1,500–$5,000 | Three campaigns (awareness → engagement → conversion), A/B test |
| $5,000+ | Full funnel with prospecting, retargeting, dynamic product ads, frequency capping |

### Campaign Objective Selection
| Goal | Objective | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Build social proof | Engagement | First campaign in sequence |
| Drive website visits | Traffic (landing page views) | When pixel has <50 weekly conversion events |
| Drive conversions | Sales | When pixel has 50+ weekly ViewContent or Purchase events |
| Collect signups | Leads (instant forms) | Event registration, Club Heaven signups |

**Note (March 2026):** The Leads objective in Meta forces Advantage+ audience mode with no way to switch to original audience. Use age (21+) and locations as hard limits. For product page campaigns, prefer Traffic or Sales objectives which allow original audience.

## Target Consumer Profile

The ideal HEAVENSAKE consumer sits at the intersection of:

### Primary Vectors (must match at least one)
1. **Japanese food/culture enthusiasts** — sushi, ramen, matcha, wagyu, Japanese craftsmanship
2. **Wine & champagne connoisseurs** — assemblage, terroir, cellar master narratives (Régis Camus connection)
3. **Health-conscious affluent consumers** — "A Better High" positioning, quality over quantity, wellness over excess
4. **Culturally refined early adopters** — art, design, travel, trend-aware, discover things first

### Secondary Vectors (amplifiers)
5. **Tech/innovation culture** — Austin, SF, Seattle tech workers, culturally curious, high-income
6. **Asian/Japanese American communities** — natural sake affinity, bridges to broader audiences

### Anti-Patterns (who NOT to target)
- Bargain hunters / deal seekers (wrong price sensitivity)
- Generic "luxury brand" followers without food/culture interest (aspirational, not buyers)
- Under-21 (legal compliance)
- National spray targeting (too diluted for niche product)
