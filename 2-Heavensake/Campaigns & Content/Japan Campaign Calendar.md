# Japan Campaign Calendar

HEAVENSAKE maps content to Japanese cultural moments. Every campaign must be asked: *What is the Heaven Sake way to do this?* Generic participation is worse than silence.

**Key principle:** Never participate in a cultural moment without a deeply meaningful, brand-authentic story. If the story doesn't exist — don't post.

## Gifting Season Priority

Sake has a very strong percentage of sales linked to gifting in Japan. Japanese buyers want to understand **WHY something is expensive** before purchasing as a gift.

**Key gifting seasons:**
1. **Ochugen** (Mid-July) — Summer gifts for superiors and mentors
2. **Oseibo** (December) — Year-end gifts for bosses, clients, family
3. **Father's Day** (June 3) — Alcohol = #1 gift category (29%)

**Gifting communication formula:**
1. Why this bottle is exceptional (craft, rarity, process)
2. Why it makes a perfect gift (prestige, uniqueness, story)
3. How to present it (packaging, card, gesture)

## Hanami — The Most Important Annual Moment

> "The whole Hanami thing is part of our DNA and we need to celebrate that in a cool way every year." (Call 26)

**2026 approach:** Small tasteful booth near Nakameguro Riverside via Heaven nightclub connection. Research needed for selling alcohol on the street regulations.

**2027 vision:** Silent disco in Yoyogi Park.
- DJ audio streamed to attendees' AirPods via HEAVENSAKE account login
- Bottles from existing park kiosk
- Drone coverage
- This is the big dream — plan now, execute in 2027. (Call 19)

The Sakura Prestige bottle was designed for this moment. Every year, Hanami is HEAVENSAKE's most authentic brand expression.

## Campaign Execution Rules

For every campaign in this calendar:

1. **Ask the Heaven Sake Way test:** Is this how a luxury cultural brand participates in this moment?
2. **Develop the story first:** What is HEAVENSAKE's unique, authentic angle?
3. **Plan content before the moment:** Launches require website/ordering readiness. Never run a viral campaign without a fully functional Japan e-commerce site. (Call 19)
4. **Tag everyone:** Tag breweries, venues, collaborators — this is courtesy AND discovery. (Call 10)
5. **Optimize for two readers:** Opening lines for skimmers; full caption for readers. (Call 16)
6. **SEO-optimize captions:** Align caption keywords with the cultural moment's search terms. (Call 16)

## Women's Day — Specific Guidance

Women's Day is about the historical oppression and fight of women. A luxury sake brand cannot participate authentically without genuine conceptual depth.

**Rejected approach:** Generic "celebrate women" content with a pink bottle.

**Approved approach (if participating):** Celebrate real women doing real work connected to HEAVENSAKE — specifically [[Nanami Watanabe]] (female Toji at Niizawa brewery), Mami San (calligrapher), and the female artisan who applies the sanded pink glaze at Arita. These are real women with real stories.

> "Please remove this and don't do this again to me please." (Call 10) — Carl's response to a generic Women's Day post.

## Valentine's Day — Specific Guidance

**Not "lovey-lovey."** HEAVENSAKE celebrates love for art, for craft, for something that sometimes transcends human love.

If participating: the scroll collaboration with [[Mami San]] is the authentic Valentine's vehicle — a handcrafted art object that represents the love of craft and the human impulse to give something beautiful.

If the story isn't there: don't participate.
