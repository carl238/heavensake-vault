# Sakura Prestige Campaign

The Sakura Prestige campaign is HEAVENSAKE's spring 2026 hero campaign — a limited edition pink ceramic bottle (Prestige 2) launched exclusively on jp.heavensake.com during Hanami season. It is simultaneously a product launch, a Japan e-commerce launch, and a cultural statement.

---

## What the Campaign Is

**The product:** Prestige 2 — a pink ceramic/sanded bottle produced by ARITA ceramics (Matsumoto-san) in collaboration with Niizawa brewery.

**Why it exists:** Originally conceived as a Women's Day campaign. Carl pivoted:

> "The news is that just randomly having a sort of a pink bottle for Women's Day is a bit like — it's too random. There's not really a concept. What it then generates as a conclusion is actually a better plan… We're missing our opportunity to use this in Japan for the launch of our e-commerce there."
> — *2026-02-04 Hanami Launch & 10th Anniversary Strategy*

The Women's Day pivot became the Hanami campaign — culturally authentic, brand-true, and strategically smarter.

---

## Product Story

**The bottle:**
- Sanded/matte finish (not shiny — "the shiny bottle was a bit cheap")
- Print color matches the raw porcelain tone at the base — harmony between ink and ceramic
- Ceramic base from ARITA ceramics
- Niizawa sake inside (Prestige 2 is with Niizawa because Noguchi does not allow Prestige 1 to sell in Japan)

**Why 50–100 bottles:**
- Limited supply creates genuine scarcity
- 50 Japan + 50 US split (being confirmed with Matsumoto-san)
- Japan: exclusive to jp.heavensake.com launch
- US: events, gifting, select accounts

**Price:** ~¥70,000 in Japan (~$500 USD equivalent)

## Creative Direction

**Core narrative:** Hanami is not a marketing hook — it is who HEAVENSAKE is.

> "The whole Hanami thing is part of our DNA and we need to celebrate that in a cool way every year."
> — *2026-02-04 Hanami Launch & 10th Anniversary Strategy*

**Authentic storytelling over artistic concept:**
Carl was explicit that the Women's Day content should prioritize real people over elaborate visual concepts. This principle carries into the Sakura campaign:
- **Nanami Watanabe (Niizawa):** The female brewer whose hands create the sake — she IS the story
- **ARITA Lab staff photos:** The women who make the ceramic bottle
- **Product photography:** Studio-quality shots of the bottle itself (sent to Tokyo for professional photography; 3D renderings requested from ARITA)

**Content formats:**
- Carousel posts (product story + women's narrative)
- Video of the sake-making process (Niizawa)
- AI-amplified/upscaled product imagery
- Influencer gifting (3–5 high-profile Japan influencers)

## E-Commerce Strategy for the Launch

The Hanami bottle IS the Japan e-commerce launch. The attract-then-downsell model:

> "You attract people into your ecosystem and then you downsell or whatever it's called — when you then sell them a $100 bottle it seems like a good deal."
> — *2026-02-04 Hanami Launch & 10th Anniversary Strategy*

- The ¥70,000 bottle creates the brand halo and captures customer emails
- Those customers are then served the regular Niizawa sake (¥10,000–¥15,000) as the next purchase
- This is how HEAVENSAKE builds its Japanese customer database from scratch

**Sales mechanics:** Lottery or first-come-first-served (being decided). Bottles not available elsewhere → forces traffic to jp.heavensake.com.
