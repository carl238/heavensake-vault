# Partnerships & Collaborations

The complete map of HEAVENSAKE's cultural partnerships, organized by category. These relationships are not endorsements — they are moments where HEAVENSAKE's world and another creator's world intersect to create something that neither could produce alone. Every collaboration must carry "cultural heat."

---

## Core Philosophy: Celebrate, Don't Use

> "What we do is we celebrate the artist. We celebrate their work... You're not celebrating the artist when you do this. You are just buying someone to do the promotion of your brand, which is fair enough, but that's not very interesting to the consumer." (Call 28)

**The contrast case:** Patron Tequila telling [[Mami San]] to paint their logo. HEAVENSAKE invites the artist's authentic creative expression. The result should feel like co-creation, not a commission.

> "The scroll is something where it's a symbol for our relationship. We create something beautiful together." (Call 33)

---

## Brand as Community Curator

> "It is a way for us to give our community access to unique artists and content from Japan." (Call 23)

HEAVENSAKE's value in collaborations is curation and access — not product promotion. The brand facilitates connections without taking a cut.

> "We are not going to make any money on the actual sale of the artwork. It's purely to support a young artist and to show our dedication to help celebrate Japanese culture." (Call 23)

---

## How to Approach Partners

1. **Always contact the marketing department, not sales.** "Sales only cares about revenue; marketing cares about brand content and exposure." (Call 15)
2. **Offer content creation as the value exchange:** "We want to create a beautiful piece of content around [venue] and we want to celebrate [them]." (Call 15)
3. **Leverage the Instagram brand value:** "90% of the people have ever asked this look at our stuff and they go like, oh my God, I would love a video like this." (Call 15)
4. **Escalate to the owner if the team is unresponsive.** (Call 15)
5. **Always reach out from the official HEAVENSAKE account:** "Reach out from the Heavensake account. It's another person that gets to discover the brand." (Call 1)
6. **Personal and direct outreach:** "Hey, what up, brother? This looks really cool. I love your work." (Call 1)

---

## Core Principle: Associate with Great People

> "That's the nature of our business. We try to associate with great people and make them their networks discover Japanese sake." (Call 32)

Every partnership must give **network access** — reaching the partner's audience of quality people. This is the core KPI.

---

## Tier Decision Is Always Carl's Call

No partner should be assigned a tier without Carl's approval. The framework is a proposal; execution requires founder sign-off. (Call 32)

---

## Payment Ethics

Carl is firm: designers and artists must be paid for work even if the project is ultimately unused:

> "We will pay because he did the effort and that's it... out of principle we will pay for his time because we respect him and his work a lot." (Call 10)

---

## No Hidden Fees

Fees should be bundled, not itemized with hidden add-ons. Administrative fees, communication fees, and small extras are unacceptable if added on top of an agreed creative fee. (Call 37)

## Collaboration Vetting Framework

Every potential collaboration must answer:

1. **Does the partner have cultural heat?** (Not just prestige — heat.)
2. **Does the intersection feel authentic?** (Would HEAVENSAKE actually drink at this person's table?)
3. **Does it generate media-worthy content?** (Photography, video, a moment that travels beyond the room.)
4. **Does it avoid "papa aesthetics"?** (No wax seals, no dusty calligraphy, no souvenir-shop Japan.)
5. **Does it create a DTC conversion pathway?** (Event guests must have a path to purchase, club membership, or CRM capture.)
