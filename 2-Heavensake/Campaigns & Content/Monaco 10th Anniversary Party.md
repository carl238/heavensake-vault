## Context & History

HEAVENSAKE has hosted a Monaco Grand Prix party annually. The 2026 edition is qualitatively different:

1. **10th anniversary of the brand** — every element of the event carries anniversary meaning. Carl has consistently framed this as the culmination of a decade of brand building.
2. **Last year's party was "too crowded"** — the 2026 edition implements a structured, monetized invitation system to control quality and cover costs.
3. **New revenue model:** Rather than absorbing the full cost, the 2026 party introduces a **VIP membership system** generating revenue from committed guests.

---

## Invitation Architecture

### Two-Tier System

**Tier 1 — Casual Invites:**
- People who live near the South of France or can attend without flight/hotel logistics
- Lower barrier to entry; broader network coverage
- Submitted by all team members (10–20 names each)

**Tier 2 — High-Profile / Paid Travel Invites:**
- High-profile, brand-moving guests from US, Asia, Europe
- Heavensake covers travel for those who would move the needle most
- Must be classified carefully by each team member (casual vs. high-profile)

### VIP Membership Model
- **Commitment:** $5,000 per member = 2 invitations to Monaco
- **Target:** 30 paid members globally → **$150,000 revenue** from memberships alone
- [[Carl]]'s comment: "I need 30 people worldwide who will commit $5,000"
- This model inverts the normal cost structure for luxury events

### Trip Incentive for Buyers
- **30 Prestige bottles purchased** = economy class flight + 3 nights accommodation in Monaco
- Connects product sales directly to event access — incentivizes distributor and account purchases
