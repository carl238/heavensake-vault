## Brand DNA Insights

- **Breathing room is luxury:** Carl's spacing philosophy extends to the entire brand. Luxury means things breathe — in typography, in product design, in strategy. Overcrowding anything = mistake.
- **100 little things make harmony:** Brand identity is the accumulation of micro-decisions, each individually dismissable but collectively defining. Carl applies this to T-shirt spacing, Daruma photography, product design.
- **Cultural authenticity over geographic convenience:** The Daruma ritual must happen in Japan, at the water source, with the right people. Doing it in France would strip it of meaning.
- **Human decisions on soul matters:** AI handles execution. Humans decide what the brand stands for, what it looks like, who it speaks to.
- **Events must translate to digital:** Parties, rituals, dinners — their value is in what they generate online. Content is the product.
- **Shibari as part of the brand DNA:** Carl references HEAVENSAKE's 2016 Paris launch with shibari performance as foundational — a brand element that was underground then, now has cultural resonance via artists like Kinoko.

## Summary

This meeting is a masterclass in Carl's creative process. He simultaneously reviews micro-design details (T-shirt typography spacing) and macro strategic architecture (Daruma ritual content planning, team restructuring). His design instincts are instinctive but deeply principled — luxury breathes, authenticity requires geographic/cultural specificity, and every physical moment must translate into digital content value.

The closing of the black Daruma's eye = closing a chapter. The pink Daruma's unopened eye = the promise of what's coming. Part 1 (May, Chichiken) and Part 2 (October, Japan event) become a serialized brand narrative.

Alongside the ritual storytelling, Carl is aggressively restructuring the team around AI tools and taste. Giancarlo is out. Claire is being elevated — but held to a high standard of discipline, systems-thinking, and tool mastery. The message is clear: in the AI era, the winners are those who combine creative taste with operational execution capability.
