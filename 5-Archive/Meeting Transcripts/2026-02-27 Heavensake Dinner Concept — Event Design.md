# Heavensake Dinner Concept — Event Design — February 27, 2026

## Meeting Context
- **Date:** February 27, 2026
- **Participants:** Carl Hirschmann, Lisa Hanze (Ruby Carmen), Odrik Giordano (Turbo Monaco), Axelle (Turbo Monaco), MOA Projects, Geert Praet
- **Duration:** 45 min
- **Organizer:** carl@heavensake.com

## Key Takeaways
The team proposed an innovative dining experience with a floating platform designed to sit just below water level, allowing versatile use for dinner or pre-event activities. The platform will support up to 60 people / 10,000 kg. A Torii gate will serve as the artistic centerpiece, symbolizing spiritual transition and aligning with the brand identity. Budget cap established at €100,000, with sponsorship sought for premium features. The construction plan emphasizes safety and visual appeal. Next steps: dual proposals for review and sponsor outreach for ambitious elements.

## Carl's Thinking
- The dinner concept is a flagship brand experience moment — not just an event but a statement about Heavensake's identity and vision.
- Budget cap: ~€100K (Carl communicated "under €150K" range to give design team room). Cost-efficiency is important.
- Sponsorship is being pursued to fund the more ambitious elements (Torii gate, platform).
- Carl is providing creative direction — wants the Torii gate inspired by sake bottle shape and Japanese aesthetics with modern elegance.
- Carl is actively steering design direction and will provide feedback on proposals.

## Heavensake Decisions
- **Platform:** Floating, just below water level, capacity 60 people / 10,000 kg.
- **Torii gate:** Modern elegant style; possible inspiration from sake bottle shape and Japanese aesthetics.
- **Budget:** ~€100K cap (communicated ~€100K–€150K range to design team).
- **Sponsorship:** Being pursued for premium elements.
- **Two proposals** to be developed for review: pool platform option and bench table option.
