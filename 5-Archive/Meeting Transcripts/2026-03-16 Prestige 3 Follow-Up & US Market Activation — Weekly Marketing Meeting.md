## Régis Camus: Guardian / Successor Discussion

This meeting contains Carl's most explicit thinking about the Régis Camus succession problem. It emerged from Cesar's request for brewery presence in Miami, which Carl expanded into a strategic conversation about who carries Régis's heritage forward.

### The Régis Problem — Carl's Assessment

> "The problem with Regis is not the money. The problem is that he's old and he doesn't want to go to the US anymore so I can try to see but. And he doesn't speak English and in general was nice to have and it generated nice relationships but he's just not the same kind of promoter and driver as the other guy."

> "Regist needs to be at some point replaced. He says it himself."

### Carl's Vision for the Guardian Role

> "Is it that we choose somebody in the US that becomes the guardian of Regist's legacy. We're going to do an assemblage maybe before the party with Kurosaki san. So yeah maybe that's an idea. Somebody in the US that could travel around the US and you know go to different places and come to Japan and witness the process and go to Champagne now in June with Regis and me and Kurosaki san — then come to Japan for Regista's last then — and then that person becomes the guardian."

> "You have to tell me who that guy or that woman would be. Somebody that becomes for me — they can even become a shareholder if we think that there's somebody that is a perfect match. They need to become something like a co-founder or a — taking on the heritage of Regis who is going into retirement. It has to be a credible story. Let's make them an offer that they can't refuse."

### Profile Definition — What Carl Is Looking For

> "Somebody that is now maybe from the wine space of champagne space in the US and that has the liberty to work for a sake brand because they are not yet connected to the sake world, but they are from the wine world or champagne world in the US and they get to be like, well, you know what? Actually like this was a life changing experience. Assemblage and now I have decided to also dedicate part of my life to promoting sake. And because they are so big and powerful or credible in wine, people will meet them."

**Key requirements Carl specifies:**
- Cannot already work for a sake brand
- From the wine/champagne world in the US (not European — they won't travel and it's not efficient)
- Has the credibility that when someone says "this person is in town" — the sommelier/wine director says "yes, I absolutely want to meet them"
- Has "liberty" — not tied to LVMH or another major house
- Possibly a woman (Carl is genuinely open to this, defers to the US team on what the market responds to)
- Could become a shareholder if the fit is right

**The invitation process Carl envisions:**
> "We're going to reach out to all of them and invite them to Champagne for a blending session with Regis and to the party and to Japan. And the goal is for them to become so mesmerized by the whole thing that they say, you know what? This needs to be shared. Because also our assemblage is completely different than the one from Risha as a concept."

### Carl Using AI to Find the Guardian

> "Ask your chat GPTs and your whatever you have and whatever or maybe I can do that myself also and try to see — that's the mission. You give me the recommendations of what you want. It will find me the top 20 people. Their LinkedIn. I can even tell it — look, they cannot work for a sake brand already. They cannot do this. And you tell me what you want them to have and what you do not want them to have and I will. And they will come up with the list and it will maybe even do the average — you reach to them automatically and that's it. It's a numbers game."

### Zak Gross's Input on Profile
- Comparable to Rashard (Dom Perignon ambassador): charismatic, speaks English, legendary brand name behind them
- When they're in a restaurant: the sommelier and wine director immediately say "yes, I want to meet this person"
- Not a champagne maker from Europe (too expensive, won't travel enough, tied to LVMH)

## Summary

This weekly marketing meeting is one of the most strategically dense in the vault — it covers Prestige 3 production updates, the David Grutman access problem, the Dorsey program debate, AND the most explicit articulation of the Régis Camus succession challenge in any transcript.

Carl's concern about Prestige 3 US timing is genuine: a France party launch in June (summer, Europe) may not resonate with the US market. His instinct is that the France party creates the global tease, but the US needs its own activated moment in September with American cultural anchors (Nas, RZA) to convert that attention into commercial momentum.

The Régis guardian discussion is the most forward-looking strategic thread. Carl is thinking in generational timescales: Régis Camus (HEAVENSAKE's master blender and the human credibility behind the assemblage concept) is aging, doesn't want to travel to the US, doesn't speak English, and acknowledges he needs to be replaced. Carl's vision for the successor is strikingly specific: a US-based wine/champagne world figure with enough stature that sommeliers and wine directors will say "yes, I want to meet them" when they come to town. The person would be converted to the sake world through direct experience — attending the Champagne blending session with Régis, coming to Japan for the brewing process, attending the party — and would become so moved by the assemblage concept that they commit to championing it.

Carl's AI casting plan is characteristic: define the profile in precise terms (wine/champagne world, US-based, not yet connected to sake, not tied to a major house), feed it to Perplexity, get the top 20 candidates with LinkedIn profiles, and reach out to all of them. It's a numbers game, and the experience itself will do the selling.
