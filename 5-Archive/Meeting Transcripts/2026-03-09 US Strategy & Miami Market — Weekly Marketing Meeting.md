## Key Strategic Decisions (from Zak Gross + team, with Carl input)

### US Market Conditions 2026
- Restaurants reducing inventory; mid-tier dining under pressure; premium category still active but very selective
- Premium sake = perceived risk for buyers — must deliver visible revenue potential
- Restaurants evaluate on: margin, staff ability to sell, consumer familiarity

### By-the-Glass Strategy
- Resistance at $22–$28 by-the-glass price point but it's market-appropriate
- Key training point: HEAVENSAKE bottles are 720ml (24oz), not 25+oz like wine → 6-8 pours per bottle
- Steer accounts toward 3-4oz pours (not 5oz like wine)
- Staff incentivization: bottles, gift cards — "these guys aren't making insane amounts of money, they just want to feel supported"
- National by-the-glass activation program: staff training cards, menu copy, pairing guides, digital marketing support

### Hero Market: Miami
- Miami identified as flagship/hero market: luxury hospitality culture, high-spend clientele, influencer-driven market, strong Asian dining scene
- Influential markets: New York, LA
- Efficient secondary markets: Orange County, San Diego, Santa Barbara, Palm Beach, Orlando, Hamptons

### Competitive Landscape
- **IWA:** Heavy activation, Michelin positioning, broad fine dining outreach — but not seeing repeat orders. Strategy: "get it everywhere and orders will come" — Carl's team sees this as flawed.
- **Sakayone:** Retreating — opportunity for HEAVENSAKE to become visible premium leader
- **SOTO:** Not worth mentioning, fading. Issue: David Grutman (Groot hospitality) has stake in SOTO.

### Miami Restaurant Groups
- In: Riviera Dining Group (RDG — Cesar has 4-year relationship), Major Food Group (MFG — ZZ's members club, Japanese concept), Good Time Hotel
- Problem: Groot Hospitality Group (David Grutman) — has SOTO stake, needs backdoor strategy
- Proposed: target Groot's establishments through indirect relationship-building; make it "impossible for him not to notice what HEAVENSAKE is doing"
- Specific venues: C Spice (four-hands dinner experience/Endo omakase), Casa Nios, Good Time Hotel (day-club vibes post-Nikki Beach closing)

### 10th Anniversary as National Catalyst
- France (June party) → tease on social media → bring to US with Nas / RZA dinners in New York and Miami (September)
- The France launch + social → US September activation is not mutually exclusive
- Prestige 3 bottle retail: ~$300, communicated to Nizawa, samples in process, 2.5 month shipping window to Monaco

## Brand DNA Insights

- **Premium sake must earn its place, not just appear:** Restaurants are in a selective mode. HEAVENSAKE can't win through volume — it wins by making itself the obvious premium choice in a category where other brands are chasing breadth. The IWA comparison makes this clear: activation without repeat orders is hollow.
- **Miami as laboratory:** The concentration of luxury hospitality, influencer culture, and high-net-worth individuals makes Miami the ideal testing ground for the HEAVENSAKE experience model. What works in Miami can be systematized and exported to NYC, LA.
- **Staff are brand ambassadors, not obstacles:** Carl's team understands that restaurant staff who haven't tried HEAVENSAKE can't sell it. Staff incentivization (giving a bottle to take home) is brand-building, not just sales support.
- **Brand names must always pass the brand filter:** Even when using AI to generate strategy documents, Carl immediately identifies off-brand terminology ("Heaven hour") and corrects it. The brand voice is non-negotiable regardless of the source.
