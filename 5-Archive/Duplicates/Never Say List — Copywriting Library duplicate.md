# THE HEAVENSAKE "NEVER SAY" LIST

## Words & Phrases — Banned Outright

**"Premium"**
Never use this word. It is a lazy shortcut. Show luxury through specificity, imagery, and craft. Never label it.

**"Blend" / "Blending"** *(when describing our process)*
Blending is industrial. It is what factories do to cut costs and standardize batches. We practice Assemblage — an artistic act of elevation, not uniformity. The distinction is non-negotiable.

**"Where precision meets poetry"**
Banned. Too cliché. This is the kind of line a standard agency writes on a Tuesday afternoon without thinking.

**"Unique"** *(used lazily)*
Only acceptable when paired with specific, substantive context. Never as a standalone descriptor. Every brand calls itself unique. We prove it.

## Concepts & Tonal Directions — Never Go Here

**Old-school / "Papa" aesthetics**
No wax seals. No dusty calligraphy. No imagery that evokes a souvenir shop or a museum exhibit. We honor Japan — we do not freeze it in amber.

**Japanese clichés without a French twist**
Cherry blossoms for the sake of cherry blossoms. Mount Fuji as wallpaper. Zen platitudes that belong on a tea bag. Every reference to Japanese heritage must carry our Franco-Japanese lens.

**Heavy sake jargon without context**
Do not drop "Junmai Daiginjo" or "Toji" into copy without explaining what it means and why it matters. Jargon without context is a wall, not an invitation.

**Cocktail culture with hard spirits or sugary mixers**
Never position HEAVENSAKE alongside tequila, hard seltzers, or sugary sodas. If we mix, it is only with fresh, pure elements — green tea, matcha, citrus. The sake is always the hero.

**Desperate celebration language**
We do not chase relevance through exclamation points, hashtag bait, or breathless hype. Confidence is quiet. Luxury does not shout.

**Generic wellness language**
"Clean." "Natural." "Good for you." These are the words of supplement brands. Our purity is communicated through sensory truth — the way the liquid feels, the morning after, the absence of weight — not through a checklist.

**Random acts of marketing**
A bottle with no context is just a product. Every image, every collaboration, every piece of copy must carry a story. If there is no story, there is no post.

**No "Pompous" or "Heavy" language:**
The tone must remain "sexy" and "cool," never overly academic or stuffy.

## Format & Structure — Never Do This

**Post a bottle without context**
Context is luxury. The content is never the picture alone — it is the meaning behind the picture.

**Open with a weak, generic line**
Every piece of copy must earn attention in the first five words. If the opener could belong to any other brand, it belongs to none of them.

**Write long lists of features**
Features are for brochures. We write for people who feel before they think. Lead with sensation, follow with substance.

**Use "just" or "simply"**
These words diminish. HEAVENSAKE does not do anything simply. Every element — the water, the assemblage, the glass — carries intention.

Also banned:
- Boastful superlatives — authority is implied through facts and narrative, never claimed directly
- Passive or generic energy — every sentence must feel alive and sensory
- Academic or corporate register — HEAVENSAKE speaks like a cultured friend, never a textbook or press release

## The Ultimate Test

Before publishing anything, ask two questions:
1. Is this generic?
2. Could a standard agency have written this in an afternoon?

If the answer to either is yes — delete it. Start over. HEAVENSAKE copy must possess Pure Elevation or cultural heat. Nothing less earns the glass.
