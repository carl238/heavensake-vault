# Carl's Membership & Customer Philosophy

Carl's approach to customers and membership is a coherent philosophy, not a tactic. Its foundation: the deepest relationships create the most durable revenue, and most brands make the mistake of ignoring their existing customers in pursuit of new ones.

---

## The 20/80 Principle: Inner Circle Before Cold Acquisition

The governing rule:

> "Don't spread thin — lock in the top 20% of customers with exceptional experiences before trying to grow the base."
> — *2026-03-11 Voice Note — Membership and 20% Care Strategy*

The math Carl has calculated for HEAVENSAKE:

> "These 200 people — I know for sure we can get between $1,000 to $2,000 a year in sales out of them. That's $200 to $400,000 in sales just with managing these people well."
> — *2026-03-18 AI Marketing Strategy — Carl + Jakub*

And the broader database version:

> "Let's say of those 5,000 email addresses, there is 1,000 that are potentially good customers that would spend between $1,200 and $4,000 a month — let's say an average of $2,500 a year. That's 1,000 people. $2,500 a year. That's $250,000 just already in our database. No ads, no whatever."
> — *2026-03-20 AI Strategy & Operations — Carl + Abdel*

**The sequence this creates:**
1. AI audit of the full database (~600–689 Shopify + ClubHeaven contacts)
2. Enrich with external data: LinkedIn, Instagram, press, industry roles
3. Identify top 20% (not just by spend — by influence, potential, and relationship)
4. Serve them extraordinarily well before spending on cold acquisition

## The Club Heaven Concept: $5K Membership

The membership tier Carl has designed:

- **Price:** $5,000/year
- **Initial target:** 30 members (= $150,000 revenue)
- **Benefits:** Access to all major HEAVENSAKE global events (Monaco F1 party, Gstaad, US dinners, Japan), annual allocation of limited editions, free express shipping, personalized brand moments
- **Positioning:** Not a loyalty program — membership in a global sake movement

The critical insight on why the Monaco party is the anchor:

> "Nobody will pay 5k for 'membership benefits' alone, but they will move for access to a legendary party plus a halo of global events and allocations."
> — *2026-XX-XX E-Commerce Strategy — Monaco F1 Party*

The launch sequence:
1. Fix inner circle care flows first
2. Welcome kit: luxury 10-year anniversary box + official Monaco invitation
3. Invitation-only rollout — ClubHeaven loyal customers first
4. Only push $5K to cold audience AFTER inner circle flows are validated

## Inner Circle Care Tiers

Carl has defined three explicit tiers of customer care:

**Top 5 — Prestige Whales**
- Personal gestures: flowers on Valentine's Day, personalized notes
- Early access to Prestige 3 (possibly gifted one bottle)
- Tailored communication acknowledging past experiences (Prestige 1 issues)

**Top 50 — Champions**
- Exclusive previews
- Personal invites to events
- Check-in emails
- Treated as insiders, not as list members

**Top 200 — High Potential**
- Tailored outreach
- Special access to limited editions
- AI-enriched personalization

> "Generic newsletters are seen as obsolete compared to cheap, personalized emails written and sent at scale with AI and light human oversight."
> — *2026-XX-XX E-Commerce Strategy — Monaco F1 Party*

The WhatsApp channel for VIP members (explored in December 2025): deliberately intimate, human-scale, and non-broadcast — the opposite of email marketing.

## Diamonds in the Rough

Pure spend/recency analysis (RFM) misses HEAVENSAKE's most valuable prospects. Carl's insight:

> "Pure spend/recency misses 'diamonds in the rough' — low spend but huge influence, or one bad experience but high potential. You need external data: LinkedIn, Instagram, news articles, roles, industries."
> — *2026-XX-XX E-Commerce Strategy — Monaco F1 Party*

This is why the AI audit goes beyond Shopify data — it cross-references each customer's real-world profile to find people who are quietly powerful and haven't been properly served.

## The Monaco Rejection Problem

Carl identified a critical design flaw in the RSVP/party funnel: applying for a party and getting rejected creates brand enemies.

> "Making people 'apply for a party' and then rejecting them creates enemies of the brand, unlike lotteries for rare bottles where losing feels like bad luck, not personal rejection."
> — *2026-XX-XX E-Commerce Strategy — Monaco F1 Party*

The solution: two separate funnels
- **VIP/known contacts:** Low-friction, concierge approach — personal invite, no application needed
- **Cold audience:** Higher-friction form captures data, but framing must be designed so rejection feels like a waitlist, not a gatekeeping decision

## Fix the Foundation Before Scaling

Carl's sequence discipline: don't run cold acquisition until the foundation is right.

> "Launching cold Meta ads for 5k memberships before you can even onboard basic customers properly is misaligned — welcome emails wrong, brand story off, no clear care plan."
> — *2026-XX-XX E-Commerce Strategy — Monaco F1 Party*

The onboarding failure he identified: welcome emails start with "Assemblage" (the sake-making technique) instead of the brand's mission ("restore sake's rightful place in the world"). This must be corrected before any growth campaign.

---

## The 5-Move Mindset

Carl demands 5-move thinking from his team — not just ad → form → party, but the full journey:

1. Who sees this and why do they care?
2. What happens when they click?
3. What happens if they are selected?
4. What happens if they are not selected?
5. How are they re-engaged in both cases?

> "You repeatedly challenged '2-move' thinking and pushed for full journey design, including fallout — people rejected for the party, how they feel, how they are re-engaged."
> — *2026-XX-XX E-Commerce Strategy — Monaco F1 Party*
