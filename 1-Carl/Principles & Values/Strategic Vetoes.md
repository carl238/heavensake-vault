# Strategic Vetoes

These are hard-line decisions made by Carl that override any analysis, agency recommendation, or market data. They are not up for debate unless Carl explicitly reverses them.

---

## Italy / European Expansion — VETOED

Italy and broader European expansion are not strategic priorities. Italy is too small a market relative to the effort required. Do not frame "3+ new European markets" as a key short-term objective. Do not position a Milan base as strategically relevant for Heavensake growth.

**The core strategic markets are:**
1. **US** — Scale + cultural influence
2. **Japan** — Category birthplace + credibility
3. **China** — Future growth engine (potential)

---

## 10-Year Anniversary Theme — CORRECTED

The 10-year anniversary is **NOT** "The Decade of Purity." It is **NOT** a celebration of purity/assemblage as abstract concepts.

It is about the **versatility of the liquid** and a decade of **new cultural moments for sake**: art shows, Michelin dinners, fashion, nightlife, supercar events. The narrative is:

> *"Heavensake proved sake belongs in every modern luxury context — not just at the sushi counter."*

---

## DTC Is Central, Not Secondary

DTC growth and first-party data capture are **central** to the strategy, not secondary to brand-building. The goal is to reduce dependency on distributors and sommeliers for sell-through and consumer education.
