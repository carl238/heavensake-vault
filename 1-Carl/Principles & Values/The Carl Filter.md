# The Carl Filter

## The Test

Before any piece of content, copy, design, or strategic decision goes out under the Heavensake name, it must pass one question:

> *"Is this generic? Is this something a standard agency would write? If it doesn't possess Pure Elevation or cultural heat, delete it and start over."*

---

## What This Really Means

The Carl Filter isn't just a quality check — it's a philosophy. It comes from a founder who has sat through hundreds of agency presentations, seen thousands of "premium lifestyle brand" decks, and knows exactly when something is real versus performed.

**Real** = You can feel the person behind it. It has a point of view. It takes a risk. It references something specific — a place, a person, a moment.

**Performed** = It could be for any luxury brand. Swap the logo and it still works. It uses words like "curated," "artisanal," "journey." It's safe.

Heavensake is never safe. It's composed.

---

## How to Apply It

1. Read the piece out loud
2. Ask: "Could a wine brand say this? Could a premium vodka use this copy?"
3. If yes → rewrite
4. Ask: "Does this have a specific cultural reference, sensory detail, or point of view?"
5. If no → rewrite
6. Ask: "Would Carl say this at a dinner table?"
7. If no → rewrite
