## Luxury Positioning & Desirability

> "Luxury is not by the glass. And it's actually funny enough, if you create enough desirability for your product, the by the glass play happens by itself. There is Dom Perignon by the glass. There's Cristal by the glass in just a very specific encounter. But at some point they realize, hey well, people love this shit."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-09-22. On Magnum positioning strategy — refusing to compete on by-the-glass pricing.

> "This is not a by-glass play by definition because the people want to take the bottle home, they want to have the bottle, they want to be seen with the bottle. It's an object. It's an iconic object play."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-08-25. Defining the ceramic magnum positioning — not for bar programs but as a collector status item.

> "This is not bottle service. This is quiet luxury theater."
> — **Zak Gross**, 2026-01-12. Describing the Magnum ritual philosophy for Tier A accounts, from his ChatGPT-assisted strategy deck.

> "You need to make people horny for stuff."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-12-22. Explaining that offering personalized tarus creates desire and buys patience for production timelines.

> "My job is to get people so hyped up on this brand like Clase Azul had a moment... that people come to beg us and we do not have the allocation because we are sold out because everybody's hyping us and wants this brand."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2026-03-02. Articulating the consumer-pull vision for Heavensake's digital strategy.

> "The consumer needs to walk through the door and tell the sommelier they need to shut the fuck up because if they don't have heaven sake on their menu, that's the name of the game for me."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2026-03-02. Explaining the strategic vision: consumer demand drives trade placement, not just trade sales.

> "Normally like you go to Sotheby's and say, hey, you have such amazing rich clientele, can I please have access to it and show them my product? And they go like, yeah, you're sponsoring my event. How much you pay me? And with us it's the other way around because we have great creativity and great network."

## Market Strategy & Consumer Behavior

> "80% of your sales come from 20% of your customers. This is the level of thinking — we need to get to the bottom of what these 20% need, miss, are interested in doing specifically on the single customer level."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-12-29. Explaining why Q1 2026 must focus on nurturing existing key accounts rather than new growth initiatives.

> "In New York, you have Brooklyn Kura, you have Dasai. It's almost, you know, not a battle that you can win for many different reasons. And so it becomes even more important to have a place where you dictate a little bit the vibe and the terms."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2026-01-05. Explaining why Miami is strategically important — it's less competitive and Heavensake can establish dominance.

> "You need to brace yourself for crossing a desert again. The guys from Clase Azul, it took them 25 years. The rosé industry took 15 years."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-12-29. Carl acknowledging the alcohol market downturn and positioning Heavensake as a long-term project requiring patience.

> "People are going to drink less and they're going to drink smarter. And in the quest for drinking smarter, they will come across sake. It's a guarantee. You cannot stop this train anymore."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-07-28. Explaining why sake category growth is inevitable despite general alcohol consumption decline.

> "What everybody that is doing a 0% is getting wrong is that they're doing 0% for the palate of sake drinkers or for the storytelling to sake drinkers. Meaning you're trying to tell a sake drinker, look, this zero percent is acceptable. But 99% of people that will drink a potentially high successful 0% sake have no interest and knowledge in how it was made."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-12-29. Explaining why 0% alcohol sake is not a current priority.

> "It's another weird thing to offer. Some people may say, I'm not that much into sake, that's fine. But if you ask 10 people, you will have four or five that say, oh it's actually a cool idea. Send them over."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2026-01-05. Carl describing the shift in perception — sake is now acceptable (even desirable) at private events, unlike 5 years ago.

> "The Monaco stuff really resonated with people. Obviously it's Monaco... but there's a bit of that European global vibe in Miami and there's a bit of the same... you have the water, you have the lifestyle, you have beautiful people. It's got that vibe as well."
> — **Zak Gross**, 2026-01-26. Explaining why Monaco brand equity translates to Miami as hero market.

> "I mean, I can't tell you how many times I have the experience where I pour someone Heaven Sake or the bottle comes out and they're like, 'I have this in my fridge.' Or 'someone gave this to me as a gift.' It's really a numbers game. It's how many people we can get to have that experience over and over again."
> — **Zak Gross**, 2026-01-12. Zak and Carl discussing the brand recognition momentum building through real-life encounters.

## Operations, Systems & Execution

> "The only thing standing between us and success are discipline in our systems. Very simple. Like it's just like there's no other way."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2026-03-02. Urging team to implement CRM and contact database systems consistently.

> "The strategy at the moment is absolutely to get the cost under control because last year was an absolute shit show for various reasons."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2026-03-02. Acknowledging previous operational issues and the need to show a well-run business before raising capital.

> "It's really, really the only thing that matters at this company is execution. Like, we have the brand, we have the product, we have the breweries, we have the distribution network."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-07-28. Emphasizing execution as the primary company challenge at this stage.

> "It's a moment now to build relationships, to build distribution, to optimize operations, set up the AI and digital infrastructure, build the team, create some stability, create all the backbone. And when we're ready, we will go and press a big marketing button."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-08-18. Strategic direction during current phase of company development.

> "We need to be out there doing a lot of this and before we automate. With all the love I have for optimization, which is clear, an important mission, you cannot automate anything if you have not defined all the processes yourself first."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-07-28. Cautioning against premature automation; advocating for manual process testing first.

> "Tell me what the account is going to do for us in exchange of that. There is no other choice. This is not a moment of high growth for any alcohol brand."
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-12-22. Establishing the core POS philosophy — every item must be tied to a deal.

> "J'ai passé maintenant deux mois dans l'enfer, dans l'enfer des opérations. Je peux vous dire que pour tout le monde qui est dans les ventes ou dans le marketing... les opérations d'une entreprise sont un cauchemar absolu. C'est comme un petit clockwork. [I have spent two months in operational hell. The operations of a company are an absolute nightmare. It's like clockwork.]"
> — **Carl Hirschmann**, 2025-09-15. Announcing new COO hire and organizational transformation after operational dysfunction.

> "Nobody's really bringing anything to these calls. And, you know, we have these meetings and we have the follow ups and you know, action items that people are supposed to go and do and bring to the call, but I don't hear anything from anybody at all other than myself."
> — **Zak Gross**, 2026-03-23. Addressing team engagement and accountability on the Monday marketing calls.
