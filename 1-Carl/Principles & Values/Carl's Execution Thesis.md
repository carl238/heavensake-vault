# Carl's Execution Thesis

> **The brand is really, really strong and desired. I believe it's just a question of execution. We have: the product, the portfolio with a nice price range from $35 to $700, the brand credibility, the distribution, the history, the storytelling, the great design. We have everything it takes. Now it's just about systems and execution.**
> — Carl Hirschmann, Founder, CEO, Creative Director & CMO

---

## The Core Belief: Everything Is in Place

HEAVENSAKE has all the ingredients. This is not a brand still searching for its identity, its product-market fit, or its positioning. The hard work of building has been done. The gap is not creative or strategic — it is operational and executional.

**What HEAVENSAKE has (confirmed by Carl):**

| Asset | Status |
|-------|--------|
| **The product** | World-class sake from Japan's most decorated breweries, assembled by the world's most-awarded champagne maker |
| **The portfolio** | $35 (Junmai 12) to ~$800 (Prestige) — a complete, well-tiered range |

| **Great design** | The porcelain bottles, the label system, the visual identity — world-class |

## The Gap: Systems and Execution

> "We have everything it takes. Now it's just about systems and execution."

The gap is not brand. It is not product. It is the **operational discipline to convert what exists into consistent, scalable revenue.**

This includes:
- **CRM & email activation** — 722 contacts, 346 buyers, only 48 repeat buyers (13.9% repeat rate). The database is underactivated.
- **E-commerce systems** — the machinery to turn traffic into revenue predictably
- **AI-augmented workflows** — reducing the per-unit cost of marketing operations (see [[Carl's AI Thesis]])
- **Team capability and accountability** — the gap between what Carl knows and what the team executes (see [[Carl's Leadership Philosophy]])
- **Consistent content cadence** — five weeks without posting is unacceptable; the system must run without Carl triggering every piece

## AI as the Execution Enabler

Carl's Execution Thesis and his AI Thesis are inseparable.

> "It is clear what is about to happen... most of the procedures, especially the annoying and repetitive ones but also many, many other ones, will be optimized, managed, and driven by AI in a way that is going to change the way companies operate. We want to be at the forefront of that."
> — [[Carl's AI Thesis]]

AI is not the vision. The vision is already in place. **AI is how execution becomes systematic and scalable** — how a lean team of 1–3 people does the work of a 15-person agency (the Anthropic marketing team benchmark).

The $250K database thesis is the operational expression of this:
> "Let's say of those 5,000 email addresses, there is 1,000 that are potentially good customers... That's $250,000 just already in our database. No ads, no whatever."

No new product needed. No new market. Just **systems to extract the value that already exists.**

## The Leadership Gap

Building the execution layer requires a team that can execute at Carl's standard — not just receive direction. See [[Carl's Leadership Philosophy]] for Carl's framework for identifying who belongs on the team.

> "One interesting test is if somebody were to call you and say, 'I am quitting. I have to.' How sad would you truly be that person quit? If your answer is 'I wouldn't really care. A part of me would actually be relieved' — you have a theoretically toxic negative person in your team."

The execution gap is partly a team gap. HEAVENSAKE is actively resolving this through:
- AI tools that reduce reliance on headcount
- A higher bar for who joins and who stays
- Clear accountability structures (daily 6pm WhatsApp report, weekly alignments)

## The Benchmark

The execution standard Carl aspires to is not a traditional brand. It is:

> "I don't know if you saw the marketing of Anthropic was made by one guy. One guy."

One person with the right tools, the right brand knowledge, and the right discipline can run HEAVENSAKE's entire marketing operation. The brand is ready. The system is being built.
